# AP Calculus AB & BC — Practice Questions

AP-style practice question sets for Units 1–8 of AP Calculus, designed to be
**standalone** but complementary to the matching study guides in
[`../Study Guides/`](../Study%20Guides/).

Each unit ships as both:

- **HTML** — KaTeX-rendered, print-styled at letter size. Open in a browser.
- **PDF** — pre-rendered "save as PDF" of the HTML for easy distribution.

Both versions have:

- A **PART I — Multiple Choice** section (15–20 items) with topic labels,
  difficulty pills (`EASY` / `MEDIUM` / `HARD`), and `Calculator` / `No Calculator`
  tags matching AP exam conventions.
- A **PART II — Free-Response Questions** section (5–7 multi-part FRQs) with
  blank work areas sized to typical AP page lengths.
- For Units 6 and 7, an additional **PART III — (BC) Extensions** section that
  AB students can skip.
- A maroon name/period header and a "Topics X.Y – X.Z" scope chip in the page
  header for at-a-glance scope identification.

## Per-Unit Highlights

### Unit 1 — Limits & Continuity
Limit estimation from tables and graphs, algebraic technique selection
(factoring, conjugate, trig identities, special trig limits), Squeeze Theorem,
piecewise continuity with parameter solving, infinite limits and end-behavior
(including radical asymptotes), and IVT applications including the
"minimum number of zeros" tabular variant.

### Unit 2 — Differentiation
Limit definition (with **disguised-form** items requiring recognition of
$f'(c)$ for trig and exponential functions), differentiability boundary cases
(corner / cusp / vertical tangent — including a $f(x)=x^{2/3}$ cusp item),
power / product / quotient rules with tabular evaluation, $a^x$ derivatives,
higher-order, and motion. The 21 MC count reflects three additional items
(Q19–Q21) added during the audit pass to close coverage gaps.

### Unit 3 — Composite, Implicit & Inverse
Chain rule with depth (including chain + product), implicit differentiation
with horizontal-tangent location, inverse-function derivative formula,
inverse-trig derivatives, higher-order implicit, and a small **preview** of
Topic 4.4 Related Rates (Q15, Q16, FRQ 4) — these items synthesize the
chain + implicit machinery from this unit and are tagged
"4.4 Related Rates (Preview of Unit 4)".

### Unit 4 — Contextual Applications of Differentiation
Derivative-as-rate interpretation in context, motion (with the speeding-up
sign rule), related rates (circle area, balloon volume, ladder, cone, shadow),
linearization with concavity-based over/underestimate analysis, and L'Hôpital
on $\frac{0}{0}$, $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$, and $0\cdot\infty$ via rewriting
($x \ln x$). Q14, Q15, and FRQ 4(b) are tagged
"5.1 MVT (Preview of Unit 5)" — MVT is formally CED Topic 5.1, but tabular
related-rate FRQs cite it routinely.

### Unit 5 — Analytical Applications of Differentiation
MVT applications, EVT and the Candidates Test on closed intervals, FDT vs.
SDT classification, concavity and inflection (with sign-change verification),
curve sketching, optimization on open and closed intervals, and contextual
related rates wrap-up.

### Unit 6 — Integration & Accumulation of Change
Riemann sums with monotonicity × concavity bias prediction, properties of
definite integrals, FTC (both parts) including the **chain-rule extension**
on the upper limit, accumulation function reasoning, $u$-substitution, average
value, motion (displacement vs. distance), and the FTC + chain rule on the
**lower** limit. The **(BC) Extensions** section adds: integration by parts
with LIATE, linear partial fractions including a long-division case, and
improper integrals with both infinite limits and **interior discontinuity**
(the FRQ BC2(c) item splits $\int_0^2 (x-1)^{-1/3}\,dx$ at $x=1$).

### Unit 7 — Differential Equations
Modeling (Newton's law of cooling, exponential growth, leakage), slope-field
reading and matching, separation of variables (including
$\pm\sqrt{\,}$ branch selection from initial conditions), exponential growth
and decay applications, and "largest open interval of validity" reasoning.
The **(BC) Extensions** section adds: Euler's method (computational and
concavity-based bias prediction) and the logistic model (carrying capacity,
$K/2$ inflection, $\lim_{t\to\infty}P(t)$ reasoning, and tangent-line
approximation comparison).

### Unit 8 — Applications of Integration
Average value, motion (displacement vs. total distance with sign-changing
$v(t)$), accumulation in applied contexts, area between curves (functions of
$x$ and of $y$), multi-intersection area, volumes via cross-sections (squares,
triangles, semicircles), and volumes of revolution via disc and washer methods
about both standard ($x$- or $y$-) and shifted ($y=k$ or $x=k$) axes. AB-only:
arc length (Topic 8.13) is BC and is correctly excluded from this set.

## Conventions & Tags

- **Difficulty pills** are color-coded: green `EASY`, gold `MEDIUM`, red `HARD`.
  Each MC item carries one. FRQ items also carry difficulty.
- **Calculator policy** is shown on every item: blue `Calculator` or grey
  `No Calculator`. The mix mirrors AP Section I conventions.
- **Topic chip** on each item names the CED sub-topic (e.g., "4.4 Related
  Rates", "6.13 Improper") so a student can map every miss back to the
  correct study-guide section.
- **Preview chip** (sand-tan) tags forward-reference items: "Preview of Unit 4"
  on the Unit 3 related-rates items, "Preview of Unit 5" on the Unit 4 MVT
  items. A short italic note inside the prompt explains the cross-reference.
- **BC chip** (purple) on every Part III item in Units 6 and 7. AB students
  may skip the entire Part III section.
- **`<` and `>` in math** use `\lt` and `\gt` (KaTeX-equivalent commands)
  inside `$...$` blocks where a literal `<` would otherwise be eaten by the
  HTML parser. This was the result of an explicit fix during the audit pass —
  patterns like `1 < x < 2` had been silently breaking before.

## Print Styling

- Body type is **Times New Roman / Times** (system fonts; no Google Font
  fetch for body text). Headers use DM Serif Display; pills use Source Sans 3.
- Page size is **letter** (`@page { size: letter }`) with 0.6 in margins.
- Tightened line-height (1.35) and font-size (11 pt) for clean PDF rendering.
- Each FRQ part has a sized work area — `.work.s` (50 px), `.work.m` (120 px),
  `.work.l` (170 px), or `.work.xl` (240 px) — chosen to match the expected
  solution length.

## Building / Local Development

Like the study guides, each `.html` file is fully self-contained. External
dependencies are limited to:

- Google Fonts (DM Serif Display, Source Sans 3, JetBrains Mono on some files)
- KaTeX 0.16.9 from jsDelivr

The PDFs in this folder were generated by browser "save as PDF" on the matching
HTML; regenerate by opening the HTML in Chrome/Edge and using the print dialog
with letter-size paper, default margins, and "Background graphics" enabled.
