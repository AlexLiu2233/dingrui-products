VERDICT: The AB Unit 1-8 corpus is COMPREHENSIVE and well-structured.
         CED coverage is essentially complete across all 8 units.
         The biggest systemic gap is CRAM-LAYER UX, not content.
         BC labeling in Units 6, 7, 8 (where it matters) is GOOD overall,
         with one practice-set gap in Unit 7.

CROSS-CUTTING STRENGTHS
  + Every unit has a flashcard section + a checklist + a Common Mistakes block.
  + BC sub-topics in Units 6 (6.11, 6.12, 6.13), 7 (7.5, 7.9), and 8 (8.13)
    are explicitly tagged with "BC Only" / "bc-tag" badges in the study guide.
  + Practice problem files correctly scope to "(AB)" in their headers.
  + No shell method appears anywhere (correctly excluded - not in CED).
  + FRQ-style "justify"/"interpret"/"set up but do not evaluate" framing is
    present in most units.

CROSS-CUTTING WEAKNESSES
  - No unit has a true "60-second cram card" / "1-hour study path" up top.
    Crammers must scroll through full sections before hitting the flashcards.
  - Several CED-required derivations are absent (sin/cos limit definition;
    derivative of arcsin via implicit diff; derivative of a^x;
    arc-length formula derivation).
  - 4.6 in the Unit 4 practice file is mis-labeled as "MVT" - MVT is CED 5.1
    (Unit 5), not Unit 4. See Unit 4 finding below.
  - Practice files for BC-mixed units (esp. Unit 7) do NOT include any
    BC-only items. AB-only is fine, but BC students lose practice surface.
  - Unit 8 study guide is unusually short (619 lines) given it covers 13
    CED topics; sections 8.1-8.3 are paragraph-thin with few worked
    examples relative to 8.4-8.12.
