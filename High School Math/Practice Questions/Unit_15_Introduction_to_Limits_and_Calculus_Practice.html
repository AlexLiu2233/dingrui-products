Q1EASYHonors 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §1 Intuitive Limits · AP Calc AB Unit 1 [3 marks]

The table below gives selected values of a function $f$ near $x = 2$:

$x$:   $1.9$   $1.99$   $1.999$    $2.001$   $2.01$   $2.1$
$f(x)$: $4.41$   $4.9401$   $4.994001$    $5.006001$   $5.0601$   $5.61$

Which value best estimates $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 2} f(x)$?

  1. (A) $4$
  2. (B) $5$
  3. (C) $5.006$
  4. (D) The limit does not exist.
Q2EASYHonors 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §2 Algebraic Limits · AP Calc AB Unit 1 [3 marks]

Evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^{2} - 9}{x - 3}$.

  1. (A) $0$
  2. (B) $3$
  3. (C) $6$
  4. (D) The limit does not exist (the function is undefined at $x = 3$).
Q3MEDIUMHonors 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §5 Power Rule · AP Calc AB Unit 2 [3 marks]

If $f(x) = 4 x^{5} - 3 x^{2} + 7 x - 11$, then $f'(x) = $

  1. (A) $20 x^{4} - 6 x + 7$
  2. (B) $20 x^{5} - 6 x^{2} + 7$
  3. (C) $4 x^{4} - 3 x + 7$
  4. (D) $20 x^{4} - 6 x + 7 - 11$
Q4MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §3 One-Sided Limits & Continuity · MCV4U Strand A [6 marks]

Consider the piecewise function $$f(x) \;=\; \begin{cases} 2x + 1, & x < 1 \\ 5, & x = 1 \\ x^{2} + 2, & x > 1 \end{cases}$$

(a) Compute the left-hand limit $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1^{-}} f(x)$ and the right-hand limit $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1^{+}} f(x)$. [2]
(b) State whether $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} f(x)$ exists, and justify in one line. [2]
(c) State whether $f$ is continuous at $x = 1$, and identify which of the three continuity conditions fails (if any). [2]
Q5MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §3 Limits at Infinity · BC Calc 12 [7 marks]

Consider the rational function $g(x) = \dfrac{3 x^{2} - 5 x + 1}{2 x^{2} + 4}$.

(a) Compute $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to \infty} g(x)$ by dividing numerator and denominator by the highest power of $x$ in the denominator. Show the dominant-term step. [3]
(b) Compute $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to -\infty} g(x)$. State whether the answer differs from part (a) and why. [2]
(c) State the equation of the horizontal asymptote of $y = g(x)$. [2]

Q6MEDIUMHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 ON AP-feeder FRQ §4 Derivative from Definition · AP Calc AB Unit 2 / MCV4U Strand B [9 marks]

Let $f(x) = x^{2} - 4 x + 5$.

(a) Write the limit definition of the derivative $f'(x)$. [1]
(b) Compute $f(x + h)$ in expanded form, then form the difference quotient $\dfrac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}$ and simplify so that the factor of $h$ in the numerator cancels cleanly. [4]
(c) Take the limit as $h \to 0$ to obtain $f'(x)$. [2]
(d) Verify your answer by applying the power rule directly to $f(x)$. Then evaluate $f'(3)$ and interpret it as the slope of the tangent line to $y = f(x)$ at the point $(3, f(3))$. [2]
Q7MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §5 Power Rule & Linearity · MCV4U Strand B [8 marks]

Differentiate each function with respect to $x$. State the power rule, sum / difference rule, and constant-multiple rule as you use them. No need to simplify beyond combining like terms.

(a) $p(x) = 6 x^{4} - 2 x^{3} + 9 x - 14$. [2]
(b) $q(x) = \dfrac{5}{x^{2}} + 8 \sqrt{x}$. (Rewrite with rational exponents first.) [3]
(c) For $p(x)$ in part (a), find the equation of the tangent line at $x = 1$. [3]
Q8HARD 🇨🇦 BC 🇨🇦 AB BC Provincial-style §2 Algebraic Limits · BC Calc 12 / AB Math 31 [8 marks]

Evaluate each limit. State the technique (direct substitution, factor and cancel, or conjugate) before computing.

(a) $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 4} \frac{x^{2} - 16}{x^{2} - x - 12}$. [3]
(b) $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{x + 9} - 3}{x}$ (use the conjugate). [3]
(c) $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^{3} - 8}{x - 2}$ (recognize the difference-of-cubes pattern). [2]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §3 Continuity at a Parameter · AP Calc AB Unit 1 / BC Calc 12 [11 marks]

Consider the piecewise function $$h(x) \;=\; \begin{cases} \dfrac{x^{2} - a^{2}}{x - a}, & x \ne a \\ b, & x = a \end{cases}$$ where $a$ and $b$ are real parameters.

(a) For $x \ne a$, simplify $\dfrac{x^{2} - a^{2}}{x - a}$ by factoring the numerator and cancelling. [2]
(b) Compute $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} h(x)$ in terms of $a$. [2]
(c) State the three conditions for continuity of $h$ at $x = a$. Use them to determine the value of $b$ (in terms of $a$) that makes $h$ continuous at $x = a$. [3]
(d) Take $a = 5$. With the value of $b$ from part (c), classify the original "hole" at $x = 5$ as either a removable discontinuity (now removed) or an essential discontinuity, and justify in one line. [2]
(e) Sketch (in your work area) the graph of $h$ for $a = 5, b = 10$, showing the line $y = x + 5$ with the point $(5, 10)$ filled in. State the slope of the line. [2]

Q10MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §4 Tangent Line & Instantaneous Rate · MCV4U Strand A & B [10 marks]

A drone's height above the ground, in metres, after $t$ seconds is modeled by $s(t) = -5 t^{2} + 40 t$ for $0 \le t \le 8$.

(a) Compute the average rate of change of $s$ on the interval $[1, 3]$, with units. [2]
(b) Compute the instantaneous rate of change $s'(t)$ using the power rule, and evaluate $s'(2)$. Interpret $s'(2)$ in context, with units. [3]
(c) Find the time at which the drone's instantaneous vertical velocity is zero. Interpret the result in context (peak height). [2]
(d) Find the equation of the tangent line to $y = s(t)$ at $t = 1$, in slope-intercept form. [3]
Q11MEDIUMHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §6 Antiderivative & Indefinite Integral · AP Calc AB Unit 6 [11 marks]

A particle moves along a straight line with velocity $v(t) = 3 t^{2} - 4 t + 2$ (in metres per second) at time $t$ (in seconds). The particle's position at time $t = 0$ is $s(0) = 5$ metres.

(a) Find the general antiderivative $s(t)$ of $v(t)$, including the constant of integration $C$. State the power rule applied in reverse: $\displaystyle\int x^{n} \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$ for $n \ne -1$. [3]
(b) Use the initial condition $s(0) = 5$ to determine $C$, and write the particular antiderivative $s(t)$. [2]
(c) Verify by differentiation that $s'(t) = v(t)$ for your particular antiderivative from part (b). [2]
(d) Compute the indefinite integral $\displaystyle\int \left( 4 x^{3} - \frac{6}{x^{2}} + 5 \right) dx$. (Rewrite $1/x^{2}$ as $x^{-2}$ before applying the reverse power rule. Do not forget $+ C$.) [4]
Q12HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §7 Definite Integral as Signed Area · AP Calc AB Unit 6 / FTC [11 marks]

Consider the function $f(x) = 4 - x^{2}$ on the interval $[-2, 3]$.

(a) Sketch the graph of $y = f(x)$ over $[-2, 3]$ in your work area. Mark the $x$-intercepts and shade the region between the graph and the $x$-axis on this interval. [2]
(b) The definite integral $\displaystyle\int_{a}^{b} f(x) \, dx$ represents the signed area between the graph and the $x$-axis on $[a, b]$ (area above the axis counts positively; area below counts negatively). Split the interval $[-2, 3]$ at the $x$-intercept $x = 2$ and describe in words the sign of the area on $[-2, 2]$ and on $[2, 3]$. [2]
(c) Using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, compute $\displaystyle\int_{-2}^{2} (4 - x^{2}) \, dx$ via an antiderivative $F$ of $f$ and the rule $\int_{a}^{b} f \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$. [3]
(d) Compute $\displaystyle\int_{2}^{3} (4 - x^{2}) \, dx$ the same way. The result should be negative, confirming the signed-area picture from part (b). [2]
(e) Combine parts (c) and (d) to compute $\displaystyle\int_{-2}^{3} (4 - x^{2}) \, dx$. State whether the result represents "net signed area" or "total geometric area between the curve and the $x$-axis", and explain in one line. [2]
