Q6MEDIUMHonors 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §3 Sum & Difference · HSF-TF.C.9 (+) [8 marks]

Use the sum and difference formulas to evaluate exact values of trigonometric expressions, expressing each answer as a single simplified radical fraction (no decimals).

(a) Compute $\cos 15^{\circ}$ exactly by writing $15^{\circ} = 45^{\circ} - 30^{\circ}$. State the formula used. [3]
(b) Compute $\sin\!\left(\dfrac{7\pi}{12}\right)$ exactly by writing $\dfrac{7\pi}{12} = \dfrac{\pi}{3} + \dfrac{\pi}{4}$. [3]
(c) Verify that $\sin(\alpha + \beta) + \sin(\alpha - \beta) = 2 \sin \alpha \cos \beta$ by expanding both terms with the sum and difference formulas. [2]
Q7HARDHonors 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §4 Double-Angle · MHF4U Trig Functions [9 marks]

Suppose $\sin \theta = \tfrac{4}{5}$ with $\theta$ in Quadrant II.

(a) Determine the exact value of $\cos \theta$ using the Pythagorean identity. Justify the sign from the quadrant. [2]
(b) Use the double-angle formula $\sin 2\theta = 2 \sin \theta \cos \theta$ to compute $\sin 2\theta$ exactly. [2]
(c) Use a suitable form of $\cos 2\theta$ to compute $\cos 2\theta$ exactly. State which of the three equivalent forms you chose and why. [3]
(d) Determine the quadrant in which the angle $2\theta$ terminates, and confirm your answers in (b) and (c) carry the right signs for that quadrant. [2]
Q8HARDHonors 🇨🇦 AB AB Provincial-style §6 Verifying Identities · Math 30-1 GO 6 (6.4) [9 marks]

Verify each identity by working from a single side. Document the strategy chosen (reduce-to-sin-cos, common denominator, factoring, or conjugate). Conclude with AG (answer given) once both sides match.

(a) Verify $\;\tan \theta + \cot \theta \;=\; \sec \theta \csc \theta\;$ by reducing the LHS to $\sin / \cos$ form and combining with a common denominator. [3]
(b) Verify $\;\dfrac{1 - \cos \theta}{\sin \theta} \;=\; \dfrac{\sin \theta}{1 + \cos \theta}\;$ by multiplying the LHS numerator and denominator by the conjugate $1 + \cos \theta$, then applying the Pythagorean identity. [3]
(c) Verify $\;\dfrac{\sin^{2} \theta - \cos^{2} \theta}{\sin \theta - \cos \theta} \;=\; \sin \theta + \cos \theta\;$ (for $\sin \theta \ne \cos \theta$) by factoring the LHS numerator as a difference of squares. [3]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §5 Half-Angle · HSF-TF.C.9 (+) / BC PC 12 enrichment [9 marks]

The half-angle formulas state $\cos\!\left(\dfrac{\theta}{2}\right) = \pm \sqrt{\dfrac{1 + \cos \theta}{2}}$ and $\sin\!\left(\dfrac{\theta}{2}\right) = \pm \sqrt{\dfrac{1 - \cos \theta}{2}}$, with the sign determined by the quadrant of $\theta/2$.

(a) Use the half-angle formula to compute $\cos 22.5^{\circ}$ exactly. Justify the choice of sign from the quadrant of $22.5^{\circ}$. [3]
(b) Suppose $\cos \theta = -\tfrac{7}{25}$ with $\theta$ in Quadrant II. Determine the quadrant of $\theta/2$, then compute the exact value of $\sin(\theta/2)$. [4]
(c) Derive the half-angle formula for $\cos$ from the double-angle identity $\cos 2x = 2 \cos^{2} x - 1$ by substituting $x = \theta/2$. State each algebraic step. [2]

Q10MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §7 Quadratic Trig Equation · PC 12 Trig [9 marks]

Solve each second-degree trigonometric equation on the restricted interval $0 \le x < 2\pi$. Show the substitution (e.g. let $u = \sin x$), factor or apply the quadratic formula, then back-substitute and list every solution.

(a) $2 \sin^{2} x - \sin x - 1 = 0$. [4]
(b) $2 \cos^{2} x = 1$. State the four solutions. [3]
(c) State the general solution to (b) in radians, using parameters $k \in \mathbb{Z}$. [2]
Q11HARDHonors 🇨🇦 AB AB Provincial-style §7 Identity-Required Equation · Math 30-1 GO 5 (5.6) [9 marks]

Some trigonometric equations cannot be solved until a Pythagorean or double-angle identity converts the expression to a single trig ratio. Solve each equation on $0 \le x < 2\pi$, citing the identity used at the conversion step.

(a) $\cos 2x + \sin x = 0$. (Hint: replace $\cos 2x$ with the form $1 - 2 \sin^{2} x$, then factor.) [4]
(b) $\sin x \tan x = \sin x$. (Hint: do not divide both sides by $\sin x$, factor instead, or you will lose roots.) State the domain restriction that excludes any extraneous solution. [3]
(c) Briefly explain why dividing both sides of (b) by $\sin x$ is a mistake: name the lost roots and the algebraic reason. [2]
Q12HARD 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §7 Modeling with Trig Equations · HSF-TF.B.7 (+) [10 marks]

A Ferris wheel of radius $10$ m has its centre at a height of $12$ m above the ground. It rotates at a constant rate, completing one full revolution every $40$ seconds. A rider boards at the lowest point at time $t = 0$ s. The rider's height above the ground, in metres, after $t$ seconds is modeled by $$ h(t) \;=\; 12 \;-\; 10 \cos\!\left(\dfrac{\pi t}{20}\right). $$

(a) Verify that $h(0) = 2$ m (the boarding platform height) and that the maximum height occurs at $t = 20$ s. State the maximum height. [2]
(b) Determine all times $t$ in the first revolution ($0 \le t < 40$) at which the rider is exactly $17$ m above the ground. Express each answer in seconds, exactly. [4]
(c) State the general solution for "rider at height $17$ m" across all rotations, using the period of $h$. Use $k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}$. [2]
(d) Explain in one sentence why the rider passes through every height between $2$ m and $22$ m exactly twice per revolution, except for the boarding height and the peak. [2]
