Q1EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1 Parabola Key Features · HSF-IF.C.7a [3 marks]

The graph of $y = (x - 3)^{2} - 4$ is a parabola in the $xy$-plane. What are the coordinates of the vertex?

  1. (A) $(-3, -4)$
  2. (B) $(3, -4)$
  3. (C) $(3, 4)$
  4. (D) $(-3, 4)$
Q2EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §3 Factoring (a = 1) · HSA-SSE.B.3a [3 marks]

Which of the following is the factored form of $x^{2} - 5x - 14$?

  1. (A) $(x - 7)(x + 2)$
  2. (B) $(x - 7)(x - 2)$
  3. (C) $(x + 7)(x - 2)$
  4. (D) $(x - 14)(x + 1)$
Q3MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §4 Discriminant · HSA-REI.B.4 [3 marks]

The quadratic equation $2x^{2} + 3x + 5 = 0$ has how many real solutions?

  1. (A) Exactly two distinct real solutions
  2. (B) Exactly one real solution (a double root)
  3. (C) No real solutions
  4. (D) Infinitely many real solutions

Q5MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §5 Completing the Square · BC PC11 quadratic functions [7 marks]

Consider the quadratic function $f(x) = 2x^{2} + 12x + 7$.

(a) Rewrite $f(x)$ in vertex form $f(x) = a(x - h)^{2} + k$ by completing the square. Show each step. [3]
(b) State the vertex, axis of symmetry, and minimum value of $f$. [2]
(c) State the domain and the range of $f$ in interval notation. [2]

(a) Convert $g(x)$ to vertex form. State the vertex and indicate whether it is a maximum or a minimum. [3]

Q8MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §4 Quadratic Formula · MCR3U A2 [9 marks]

Consider the quadratic equation $3x^{2} - 7x - 2 = 0$.

(a) Compute the discriminant $\Delta = b^{2} - 4ac$. [2]
(b) Use the discriminant to classify the number and type of roots (two distinct real, one repeated real, or two non-real complex roots). [1]
(c) Solve the equation using the quadratic formula. Give exact answers in simplified radical form. [4]
(d) Using function notation $f(x) = 3x^{2} - 7x - 2$, evaluate $f(0)$ and explain what it represents on the graph. [2]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §4 Complex Roots · HSN-CN.A.7 (+) / BC PC11 [11 marks]

Consider the family of quadratic equations $x^{2} - 4x + c = 0$, where $c$ is a real parameter.

(a) Express the discriminant $\Delta$ as a function of $c$. [1]
(b) Find the value(s) of $c$ for which the equation has exactly one real solution (a double root). State that root. [2]
(c) State the range of values of $c$ for which the equation has two distinct real solutions, and the range for which it has two non-real complex solutions. [3]
(d) Take $c = 13$ and solve the equation using the quadratic formula. Express the two complex solutions in the form $a + bi$, and verify that they are complex conjugates of each other (cite the reasoning behind HSN-CN.A.7). [4]
(e) Interpret part (d) geometrically: describe what the absence of real roots tells you about the graph of $y = x^{2} - 4x + 13$ relative to the $x$-axis. [1]

Q10MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §6 Projectile Modeling · HSF-IF.B.4 [10 marks]

A model rocket is launched vertically upward from a platform $2$ m above the ground with an initial speed of $30$ m/s. Its height above the ground, in metres, after $t$ seconds is modeled by $h(t) = -4.9 t^{2} + 30 t + 2$.

(a) State the initial height $h(0)$ and interpret it in context. [2]
(b) Determine the time at which the rocket reaches its maximum height, and the maximum height. Round each to the nearest tenth. [3]
(c) Determine the time at which the rocket strikes the ground. Round to the nearest tenth of a second. [3]
(d) State the practical (situational) domain and range of $h(t)$ in interval notation. Interpret HSF-IF.B.4 in context: list one increasing interval and one decreasing interval. [2]
Q11MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §6 Revenue Modeling · MCR3U A2.5 [11 marks]

A small bakery currently sells $200$ croissants per day at a price of CA$3.00 each. A market study estimates that, for every CA$0.10 increase in price, daily sales will drop by $5$ croissants. Let $x$ be the number of CA$0.10 price increases.

(a) Write a linear expression for the new price per croissant, $P(x)$, and a linear expression for the new daily quantity sold, $Q(x)$. [2]
(b) Write the daily revenue $R(x) = P(x) \cdot Q(x)$ in expanded standard form. [2]
(c) Determine the value of $x$ that maximizes daily revenue, and compute the maximum revenue. [4]
(d) State the optimal price per croissant and the optimal daily quantity. [2]
(e) State one restriction on the variable $x$ that arises from the situation (e.g. a non-negativity or whole-number constraint), and justify it briefly. [1]
Q12HARDHonors 🇨🇦 BC 🇺🇸 US BC Provincial-style §7 Quadratic Inequality · BC PC11 inequalities [11 marks]

Consider the quadratic inequality $2x^{2} - 5x - 12 < 0$.

(a) Factor the corresponding quadratic expression $2x^{2} - 5x - 12$, and state the roots of $2x^{2} - 5x - 12 = 0$. [3]
(b) Construct a sign chart showing the sign of $2x^{2} - 5x - 12$ on each interval determined by its roots. Label each interval with its sign. [3]
(c) State the solution set of the inequality $2x^{2} - 5x - 12 < 0$ in interval notation. [2]
(d) Without redoing the sign chart, state the solution set of the related inequality $2x^{2} - 5x - 12 \ge 0$ in interval notation. [1]
(e) A small park's profit, in hundreds of dollars, on a busy day depends on the daily admission price $x$ (in dollars) via $\Pi(x) = -2x^{2} + 5x + 12$. Use your sign-chart reasoning from (b) to state the range of admission prices $x \ge 0$ for which the park earns a strictly positive profit. [2]
