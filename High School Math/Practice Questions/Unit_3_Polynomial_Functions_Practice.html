PART I  ·  SHORT RESPONSESAT-style MCQ + ON/BC short answer · 22 marks

Section A · Short Response

Mix of multiple-choice and short-answer items. For MCQs, circle the letter; show enough work in the margin that a marker could verify. For division items, line the columns up carefully and label your quotient and remainder explicitly. No calculator on Q1–Q5.

Q1EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1 Polynomial Vocabulary · HSA-SSE.A.1 [3 marks]

Consider the polynomial $p(x) = -4x^{5} + 7x^{3} - 2x^{2} + 9$. What are its degree, leading coefficient, and constant term, respectively?

  1. (A) $5,\ -4,\ 9$
  2. (B) $5,\ 7,\ 9$
  3. (C) $4,\ -4,\ -2$
  4. (D) $9,\ -4,\ 5$
Q2EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §3 Remainder Theorem · HSA-SSE.A.2 [3 marks]

Let $p(x) = x^{3} - 4x^{2} + 5x - 7$. By the remainder theorem, what is the remainder when $p(x)$ is divided by $(x - 2)$?

  1. (A) $-5$
  2. (B) $-3$
  3. (C) $0$
  4. (D) $5$
Q3MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §5 End Behaviour · HSF-IF.B.4 [4 marks]

As $x \to +\infty$ and as $x \to -\infty$, what is the end behaviour of $f(x) = -2x^{4} + 6x^{3} - x + 8$?

  1. (A) $f(x) \to +\infty$ on both ends.
  2. (B) $f(x) \to -\infty$ on both ends.
  3. (C) $f(x) \to +\infty$ as $x \to -\infty$ and $f(x) \to -\infty$ as $x \to +\infty$.
  4. (D) $f(x) \to -\infty$ as $x \to -\infty$ and $f(x) \to +\infty$ as $x \to +\infty$.
Q4MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §2 Polynomial Long Division · MHF4U Polynomial & Rational Functions [6 marks]

Divide $p(x) = 2x^{3} + 5x^{2} - 4x + 3$ by $d(x) = x + 3$ using polynomial long division.

(a) Set up the long-division tableau and carry out every step. State the quotient $q(x)$ and the remainder $r$. [4]
(b) Write the division identity $p(x) = d(x) \cdot q(x) + r$ explicitly, with your values substituted. [2]
Q5EASY 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §2 Synthetic Division · BC PC12 polynomial functions and equations [6 marks]

Consider $p(x) = x^{3} - 6x^{2} + 11x - 6$.

(a) Use synthetic division to divide $p(x)$ by $(x - 1)$. Show the synthetic-division tableau with the divisor root, the bring-down row, and the multiply/add columns. [3]
(b) State the quotient $q(x)$ and the remainder $r$. [2]
(c) Use your remainder from (b) to state whether $(x - 1)$ is a factor of $p(x)$, citing the factor theorem by name. [1]
PART II  ·  EXTENDED RESPONSEAP-feeder FRQ + honors · 36 marks

Section B · Extended Response

Show every algebraic step. State the theorem you invoke by name: "by the factor theorem…", "by the rational root theorem the candidates are…". When sketching, label every $x$-intercept with its multiplicity and state the end-behaviour signs as a one-line conclusion. No calculator on Q6–Q9.

Q6MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §3 Factor Theorem · HSF-IF.C.8 [8 marks]

Let $p(x) = x^{3} + 2x^{2} - 5x - 6$.

(a) Evaluate $p(-1)$, $p(2)$, and $p(-3)$. [3]
(b) Citing the factor theorem by name, state which of $(x + 1)$, $(x - 2)$, and $(x + 3)$ are factors of $p(x)$. [2]
(c) Use your results from (a) and (b) to factor $p(x)$ completely over the real numbers, and state all three real zeros of $p$. [3]
Q7HARD 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §4 Rational Root Theorem · MHF4U Polynomial & Rational Functions [10 marks]

Consider the polynomial equation $2x^{3} - 3x^{2} - 11x + 6 = 0$.

(a) List the complete set of candidate rational roots given by the rational root theorem. Show how you generated the $\pm p / q$ list from the constant term and the leading coefficient. [3]
(b) Test candidates by direct substitution (or synthetic division) until you find one rational root. Identify it. [2]
(c) Use synthetic or long division to depress the cubic to a quadratic factor, and then factor the quadratic. [3]
(d) State all three real roots of the equation and the complete factorization of $2x^{3} - 3x^{2} - 11x + 6$ over the rationals. [2]
Q8MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §5 Zeros, Multiplicity & End Behaviour · HSF-IF.C.7 [9 marks]

Let $f(x) = -(x + 2)^{2}(x - 1)(x - 3)$.

(a) State the degree of $f$ and the sign of its leading coefficient. Hence state the end behaviour as $x \to \pm \infty$. [2]
(b) List every $x$-intercept of $y = f(x)$ together with its multiplicity, and state for each whether the graph crosses or touches the $x$-axis there. [3]
(c) State the $y$-intercept of the graph. [1]
(d) Sketch a smooth curve consistent with parts (a)–(c). Label every intercept and the end-behaviour arrows. [3]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §6 Complex Conjugate Roots · HSN-CN.A.7 / BC PC12 [9 marks]

A real cubic polynomial $p(x)$ has integer coefficients, leading coefficient $1$, and is known to have $x = 3$ and $x = 1 + 2i$ as two of its three roots.

(a) Using the complex conjugate root theorem (cite HSN-CN.A.7 for US, or BC PC12 polynomial-equations for BC), state the third root and justify in one sentence why it must take that value. [2]
(b) Show that the product $(x - (1 + 2i))(x - (1 - 2i))$ simplifies to the real quadratic factor $x^{2} - 2x + 5$. [3]
(c) Hence write $p(x)$ as a product of a real linear and a real irreducible quadratic factor, and expand it into standard form $p(x) = x^{3} + a x^{2} + b x + c$ with integer coefficients $a, b, c$. [3]
(d) Interpret the result geometrically: how many real $x$-intercepts does the graph of $y = p(x)$ have, and what does the irreducible quadratic factor tell you about the graph? [1]
PART III  ·  MODELING / APPLIEDUniversal · 32 marks

Section C · Modeling and Applications

Name your variables (with units) before writing equations. Set up your model, solve, and conclude with a one-sentence answer in context. Calculator permitted throughout Part III.

Q10MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §4 Polynomial Modeling · HSA-CED.A.1 [10 marks]

An open-top box is built from a $20\text{ cm} \times 16\text{ cm}$ rectangle of card by cutting a square of side $x$ cm from each of the four corners and folding up the flaps. The volume of the resulting box, in cm$^{3}$, is given by $V(x) = x(20 - 2x)(16 - 2x)$.

(a) Expand $V(x)$ into standard polynomial form and state its degree. [2]
(b) State the practical (situational) domain of $V(x)$ in interval notation, and explain in one sentence why each endpoint is excluded. [2]
(c) Solve $V(x) = 384$ algebraically using the rational root theorem to find any rational root, then factoring. Report every solution in the practical domain, rounded to the nearest tenth if irrational. [4]
(d) Pick one solution from (c) and state, in one sentence, the dimensions ($L \times W \times H$, in cm) of the resulting box. [2]
Q11MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §5 Building a Polynomial from its Zeros · MHF4U Polynomial & Rational Functions [11 marks]

A cubic polynomial function $f$ models a measured signal. The function has $x$-intercepts at $x = -2$ (a simple zero), $x = 1$ (a zero of multiplicity 2), and a $y$-intercept of $f(0) = 8$.

(a) Write $f(x)$ in factored form $f(x) = a(x - r_{1})(x - r_{2})^{m_{2}}$ with unknown leading coefficient $a$. [2]
(b) Use the condition $f(0) = 8$ to determine the value of $a$. [2]
(c) Expand $f(x)$ into standard form $f(x) = a_{3} x^{3} + a_{2} x^{2} + a_{1} x + a_{0}$. [3]
(d) State the end behaviour of $f$ as $x \to \pm \infty$, the multiplicity-based crossing/touching behaviour at each zero, and sketch a smooth curve consistent with all of the above. Label intercepts and end-behaviour arrows. [4]
Q12HARDHonors 🇨🇦 BC 🇺🇸 US BC Provincial-style §7 Polynomial Inequality (Sign Analysis) · BC PC12 / HSA-REI.D.11 [11 marks]

Consider the polynomial inequality $x^{3} - 4x^{2} + x + 6 \le 0$.

(a) Verify by direct substitution that $x = -1$ is a root of $p(x) = x^{3} - 4x^{2} + x + 6$, citing the factor theorem in one sentence. [2]
(b) Use synthetic or long division to factor $p(x)$ completely over the real numbers. State all three real zeros. [3]
(c) Construct a sign chart for $p(x)$ on the intervals determined by its zeros. Label each interval with the sign of $p$. [3]
(d) State the solution set of $x^{3} - 4x^{2} + x + 6 \le 0$ in interval notation, taking care to include the boundary zeros. [2]
(e) A small reservoir's net inflow rate, in cubic metres per hour, $t$ hours after midnight is modeled by $r(t) = -p(t) = -(t^{3} - 4t^{2} + t + 6)$ for $t \in [0, 4]$. Use your sign analysis from (c) to state the subinterval(s) of $[0, 4]$ on which water is flowing into the reservoir (i.e. $r(t) \ge 0$). [1]
