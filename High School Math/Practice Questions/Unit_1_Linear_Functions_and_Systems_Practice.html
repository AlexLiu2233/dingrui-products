Q1EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1.1 Slope · HSF-IF.B.6 [3 marks]

A line passes through the points $(-4, 7)$ and $(2, -5)$. What is the slope of the line?

  1. (A) $-2$
  2. (B) $-\tfrac{1}{2}$
  3. (C) $\tfrac{1}{2}$
  4. (D) $2$
Q2EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1.2 Slope-Intercept · HSA-REI.B.3 [3 marks]

Which of the following is equivalent to the equation $4x - 3y = 12$ written in slope-intercept form?

  1. (A) $y = -\tfrac{4}{3} x + 4$
  2. (B) $y = \tfrac{4}{3} x - 4$
  3. (C) $y = 4 x - 12$
  4. (D) $y = \tfrac{3}{4} x - 3$
Q3EASY 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §1.1 Rate of Change · MPM1D Linear Relations [4 marks]

A community pool is being drained. After $3$ minutes there are $4200$ litres of water left; after $11$ minutes there are $2600$ litres left. Assume the water level decreases at a constant rate.

(a) Compute the rate of change of volume with respect to time. Include units. [2]
(b) Interpret the sign of the rate in the context of the situation. [1]
(c) State one restriction on the variable $t$ (time) that arises from the situation. [1]
Q4MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1.2 Vertical Line · HSF-IF.C.7a [3 marks]

In the $xy$-plane, line $\ell$ passes through the points $(5, -8)$ and $(5, 17)$. Which of the following is an equation of line $\ell$?

  1. (A) $y = 5$
  2. (B) $y = -8$
  3. (C) $x = 5$
  4. (D) $y = \tfrac{25}{2} x$
Q5MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §1.3 Graphing · FMPC10 linear functions [5 marks]

Consider the line with equation $3x + 4y = 12$.

(a) Determine the $x$-intercept and the $y$-intercept of the line. [2]
(b) Sketch the line on coordinate axes, labelling both intercepts and one additional lattice point. [2]
(c) State the slope of the line and classify the slope sign (positive, negative, zero, or undefined). [1]

Q6MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §1.4 Parallel & Perpendicular · HSF-LE.A.2 [7 marks]

Let $\ell_{0}$ be the line $2x + 5y = 10$ in the $xy$-plane.

(a) Write $\ell_{0}$ in slope-intercept form and state its slope $m_{0}$. [2]
(b) Find the equation, in slope-intercept form, of the line through $(-3, 1)$ that is parallel to $\ell_{0}$. [2]
(c) Find the equation, in slope-intercept form, of the line through $(4, -2)$ that is perpendicular to $\ell_{0}$. [3]
Q7MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §1.4 Equations from a Table · MCR3U A1.2 / A1.7 [8 marks]

The table below records a linear relation between $x$ and $y$.

$x$: $\;\;-2 \quad 1 \quad 4 \quad 7 \quad 10$
$y$: $\;\;\;\;\,11 \quad 5 \quad -1 \quad -7 \quad -13$

(a) Verify, using first differences, that the relation is linear. [2]
(b) Determine the equation of the relation in the form $y = m x + b$. [3]
(c) Using function notation $f(x) = m x + b$, evaluate $f(20)$. [1]
(d) Determine the equation of the inverse function $f^{-1}(x)$ in slope-intercept form. [2]
Q8HARD 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §1.5 Linear Systems · HSA-REI.C.5 / C.6 [9 marks]

Consider the system $\begin{cases} 3x + 2y = 16 \\ 5x - 4y = 1 \end{cases}.$

(a) Solve the system by elimination, showing the row operation you apply. [3]
(b) Solve the same system by substitution and confirm that both methods give the same ordered pair. [3]
(c) Briefly justify why replacing one equation by the sum of itself and a multiple of the other does not change the solution set (cite the reasoning behind HSA-REI.C.5). [2]
(d) State the geometric meaning of the answer in terms of the two lines. [1]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC 🇨🇦 ON AP-feeder FRQ §1.7 Matrix Row-Reduction · HSA-REI.C.8/9 (+) / BC PC12 / ON MCV4U [11 marks]

Consider the $3 \times 3$ system $\begin{cases} \;\;\,x + 2y + z = 9 \\ 2x - y + z = 3 \\ \;\;\,x + y - z = 0 \end{cases}.$

(a) Write the augmented matrix $[A \,|\, \mathbf{b}]$ of the system. [1]
(b) Apply elementary row operations to bring the matrix to row-echelon form. Record each operation in the standard notation $R_{i} \to R_{i} + k R_{j}$ beside the step. [5]
(c) Back-substitute to obtain $(x, y, z)$. [3]
(d) Verify your answer by substituting back into each of the three original equations. [2]

Q10MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §1.6 Break-even · HSF-LE.B.5 [8 marks]

A student club is producing custom T-shirts to sell at a school fair. The printing service charges a one-time setup fee of US$90 plus US$6.50 per shirt. The club plans to sell each shirt for US$12.

(a) Let $n$ be the number of shirts produced and sold. Write a linear cost function $C(n)$ and a linear revenue function $R(n)$. [2]
(b) Interpret the slope and the intercept of $C(n)$ in the context of the situation (Common Core HSF-LE.B.5). [2]
(c) Find the break-even quantity $n^{*}$ algebraically. [2]
(d) State the smallest whole number of shirts the club must sell to make a profit, and give the profit at that quantity. [2]
Q11HARD 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §1.6 Mixture System · MPM2D Analytic Geometry [10 marks]

A chemistry technician needs $24$ litres of a $35\%$ alcohol solution for a lab. The stockroom has two solutions on hand: a $20\%$ alcohol solution and a $50\%$ alcohol solution. Let $x$ be the litres of $20\%$ solution and $y$ be the litres of $50\%$ solution used.

(a) Write an equation that records the total volume constraint. [1]
(b) Write an equation that records the total alcohol constraint. [2]
(c) Solve the system by substitution or elimination. Show every step. [4]
(d) Verify your answer by computing the percentage of alcohol in the resulting mixture. [2]
(e) Write a one-sentence concluding statement that includes both quantities with units. [1]
Q12MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §1.5 / §1.6 Rate System · FMPC10 systems [10 marks]

Two cyclists, Maya and Jin, leave the same trailhead and ride along the same straight forest road. Maya starts at $9{:}00$ a.m. and rides at a constant speed of $18$ km/h. Jin starts $30$ minutes later, at $9{:}30$ a.m., and rides at a constant speed of $24$ km/h. Let $t$ be the time, in hours, measured from $9{:}00$ a.m., and let $d$ be the distance, in kilometres, from the trailhead.

(a) Write a linear equation $d_{M}(t)$ giving Maya's distance from the trailhead at time $t \ge 0$. [2]
(b) Write a linear equation $d_{J}(t)$ giving Jin's distance from the trailhead at time $t \ge 0.5$, and explain the domain restriction. [3]
(c) Determine, by inspection or by solving the system $d_{M}(t) = d_{J}(t)$, the time at which Jin catches up to Maya. State the time as both a value of $t$ (in hours) and a clock time. [3]
(d) Sketch both lines on a single set of $(t, d)$ axes for $0 \le t \le 3$, marking the point of intersection and Jin's $t$-intercept. Label which line is which. [2]
