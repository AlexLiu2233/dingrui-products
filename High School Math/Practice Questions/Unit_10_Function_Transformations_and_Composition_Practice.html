PART I  ·  SHORT RESPONSESAT-style MCQ + ON/BC short answer · 18 marks

Section A · Short Response

Mix of multiple-choice and short-answer items. For MCQs, circle the letter; show enough work in the margin that a marker could verify. For short-answer items, name the parameter you are reading ($a$, $b$, $h$, or $k$) before you commit. No calculator on Q1–Q5.

Q1EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1 Shifts · HSF-BF.B.3 [3 marks]

The graph of $y = f(x + 4) - 5$ is obtained from the graph of $y = f(x)$ by which transformation?

  1. (A) Shift right $4$ units and up $5$ units
  2. (B) Shift right $4$ units and down $5$ units
  3. (C) Shift left $4$ units and up $5$ units
  4. (D) Shift left $4$ units and down $5$ units
Q2EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §3 Reflections · HSF-BF.B.3 [3 marks]

The point $(-3, 5)$ lies on the graph of $y = f(x)$. What is the corresponding point on the graph of $y = f(-x)$?

  1. (A) $(-3, -5)$
  2. (B) $(3, -5)$
  3. (C) $(3, 5)$
  4. (D) $(-5, -3)$
Q3MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §5 Even / Odd / Neither · HSF-BF.B.3 [3 marks]

Which of the following functions is odd?

  1. (A) $f(x) = x^{2} + 1$
  2. (B) $f(x) = x^{3} - 4x$
  3. (C) $f(x) = x^{3} + 2$
  4. (D) $f(x) = |x|$
Q4MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §2 Stretches (b-inside reversal) · MCR3U A1.5 [4 marks]

The point $(8, 3)$ lies on the graph of $y = f(x)$.

(a) State the corresponding point on the graph of $y = f(4 x)$, and identify whether this is a horizontal stretch or a horizontal compression. [2]
(b) State the corresponding point on the graph of $y = \tfrac{1}{2} f(x)$, and identify the vertical transformation. [2]
Q5MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC 🇨🇦 AB BC Provincial-style §1 + §3 Coordinate Rules · BC PC 12 / AB Math 30-1 RF 2.1, 5.1 [5 marks]

The graph of $y = \sqrt{x}$ is transformed in three independent ways. For each transformed graph, state the image equation and give the image of the parent-graph point $(4, 2)$.

(a) Shift the graph $3$ units to the right and $1$ unit down. [2]
(b) Reflect the graph over the $x$-axis. [2]
(c) Reflect the graph over the $y$-axis (note: the resulting graph is no longer the principal $\sqrt{x}$). [1]
PART II  ·  EXTENDED RESPONSEAP-feeder FRQ + honors · 35 marks

Section B · Extended Response

Show every algebraic step. For master-form reads, factor the $b$ out of the bracket before you state $h$. For composition, write the inner-then-outer substitution explicitly. For inverses, use the swap-and-solve method and state any domain restriction. No calculator on Q6–Q9 unless noted.

Q6MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §4 Master Form · HSF-BF.B.3 [8 marks]

Consider the function $y = -2 f\bigl(\tfrac{1}{3}(x - 6)\bigr) + 4$, where $f$ is a given parent function.

(a) Identify the four master-form parameters $a$, $b$, $h$, $k$. [2]
(b) Describe in words the four geometric transformations applied to $y = f(x)$, in the correct order of operations (horizontal first, then vertical). [4]
(c) The point $(3, 1)$ lies on $y = f(x)$. Find its image on the transformed graph. [2]
Q7HARD 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §4 Master Form, b-Factor-Out · MCR3U A1.8 [9 marks]

Consider the transformed function $y = f(2 x - 10) + 3$, where $f$ is a parent function.

(a) Rewrite $y = f(2 x - 10) + 3$ in master form $y = a f(b(x - h)) + k$ by factoring the $b$ out of the bracket. State $a$, $b$, $h$, $k$. [3]
(b) List the four transformations applied to $y = f(x)$, in order of operations. Be explicit about whether the horizontal compression precedes or follows the horizontal shift, and explain why. [3]
(c) The parent graph passes through $(0, 0)$, $(2, 4)$, and $(4, 0)$. Find the images of these three points on the transformed graph. [3]
Q8MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC 🇨🇦 AB BC Provincial-style §7 Inverses (swap-and-solve) · AB Math 30-1 RF 6.1–6.5 [9 marks]

Let $f(x) = 3 x - 5$ and $g(x) = (x - 1)^{2} + 2$.

(a) Determine $f^{-1}(x)$ using the swap-and-solve method. State its domain and range. [3]
(b) Verify algebraically that $f(f^{-1}(x)) = x$ for all $x$ in the domain of $f^{-1}$. [2]
(c) Explain why $g$ does not have an inverse on its natural domain. State a domain restriction for $g$ that produces a one-to-one function, and find $g^{-1}(x)$ on that restricted domain. [4]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 ON AP-feeder FRQ §6 Composition · HSF-BF.A.1.c (+) / MHF4U Strand C [9 marks]

Let $f(x) = \sqrt{x - 1}$ and $g(x) = x^{2} - 5$.

(a) Compute $(f \circ g)(3)$ and $(g \circ f)(10)$. Show the inner-then-outer substitution explicitly. [2]
(b) Find an explicit formula for $(f \circ g)(x)$, and state its domain. (Hint: the domain restriction is "the inner output must be a legal input to the outer".) [3]
(c) Find an explicit formula for $(g \circ f)(x)$, and state its domain. [2]
(d) Demonstrate by counter-example that composition is not commutative: produce an input $x$ for which $(f \circ g)(x) \ne (g \circ f)(x)$. [2]
PART III  ·  MODELING / APPLIEDUniversal · 28 marks

Section C · Modeling and Applications

Name your variables (with units) before writing equations. Set up your model, solve, and conclude with a one-sentence answer in context. Calculator permitted throughout Part III.

Q10MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §4 Master Form on $\sqrt{x}$ · HSF-BF.B.3 [9 marks]

Let $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$ (the parent square-root function), and define $T(x) = 2 \sqrt{x - 3} + 1$.

(a) Express $T(x)$ in master form $y = a f(b(x - h)) + k$, and state $a$, $b$, $h$, $k$. [2]
(b) List the geometric transformations applied to $f$, in order of operations. [2]
(c) State the domain and the range of $T$ in interval notation. Justify your answer by tracking the parent's domain and range through each transformation. [3]
(d) The parent function passes through $(0, 0)$, $(1, 1)$, and $(4, 2)$. Find the images of these three points on the graph of $T$. [2]
Q11MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §6 + §7 Composition + Inverse · MCR3U A1.4 / MHF4U C [9 marks]

A Canadian ski resort posts temperature in degrees Celsius and altitude in metres. Let $C(a) = 12 - 0.0065 \, a$ be the air temperature in °C at altitude $a$ metres above the base (a standard atmospheric lapse-rate model). A US visitor wants the temperature in °F. The Fahrenheit conversion is $F(c) = \tfrac{9}{5} c + 32$.

(a) Write a composite function $H(a) = (F \circ C)(a)$ giving the temperature in °F as a function of altitude in metres. Simplify to a linear expression in $a$. [3]
(b) Use $H(a)$ to find the Fahrenheit temperature at altitude $a = 2000$ m. [2]
(c) Find $H^{-1}(F)$ using the swap-and-solve method; state what its output represents in context. [3]
(d) Use $H^{-1}$ to find the altitude (to the nearest metre) at which the air temperature is $32^{\circ}\mathrm{F}$. Interpret in one sentence. [1]
Q12HARDHonors 🇨🇦 BC 🇺🇸 US BC Provincial-style §4 + §7 Synthesis on a Cubic · BC PC 12 / HSF-BF.B.4 [10 marks]

Let $f(x) = x^{3}$ (the parent cubic), and define $T(x) = -2(x + 1)^{3} + 4$.

(a) Express $T(x)$ in master form $y = a f(b(x - h)) + k$, and state $a$, $b$, $h$, $k$. [2]
(b) Describe the four geometric transformations applied to $f$, in order. [2]
(c) The parent cubic $f(x) = x^{3}$ is a one-to-one odd function on its full domain. Explain in one or two sentences why $T$ remains one-to-one (so $T^{-1}$ exists on all of $\mathbb{R}$). [1]
(d) Find $T^{-1}(x)$ using the swap-and-solve method. State the domain and the range of $T^{-1}$. [4]
(e) Verify your inverse by evaluating $T(T^{-1}(4))$. [1]
