Q7MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style Honors / Pre-Calc §4 Sine Graph Features · MHF4U Strand B [8 marks]

Consider the function $f(x) = \sin x$ on the closed interval $[0, 2\pi]$.

(a) List the five "key points" of one full period at $x = 0, \tfrac{\pi}{2}, \pi, \tfrac{3\pi}{2}, 2\pi$ with their function values $\bigl(x, \sin x\bigr)$. [2]
(b) State the amplitude, period, and range of $f$. [3]

Q8HARD 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style Honors / Pre-Calc §7 Transformations · BC PC 12 [8 marks]

Consider $g(x) = 3\sin\!\left(2\!\left(x - \dfrac{\pi}{4}\right)\right) - 1$.

(a) Identify $A$, $B$, $C$, and $D$ in the transformation form $y = A\sin\bigl(B(x - C)\bigr) + D$. [1]
(b) State the amplitude, period, phase shift, and vertical shift, with appropriate signs and direction (e.g. "phase shift $\tfrac{\pi}{4}$ to the right"). [3]
(c) State the maximum value, minimum value, and the equation of the midline (sinusoidal axis). [3]
(d) State the range of $g$ in interval notation. [1]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §7 Inverse Modeling · HSF-TF.B.5 / BC PC 12 & AB Math 30-1 GO 4.9 [11 marks]

A sinusoidal curve passes through the maximum point $\left(\dfrac{\pi}{6}, 7\right)$ and the next minimum point $\left(\dfrac{7\pi}{6}, -3\right)$. The curve is to be written in the form $y = A\sin\!\bigl(B(x - C)\bigr) + D$ with $A > 0$ and $B > 0$.

(a) Determine the midline equation $y = D$ from the average of the max and min heights, and state $D$. [1]
(b) Determine the amplitude $A$ from the half-distance between max and min. [1]
(c) The distance from a max to the next min is half a period. Use this to determine the period $T$, then the angular frequency $B = \tfrac{2\pi}{T}$. [3]
(d) The parent function $\sin$ reaches its max at $x = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$. For the transformed curve, the max occurs where $B(x - C) = \tfrac{\pi}{2}$. Use the given max coordinate to solve for $C$. [3]
(e) Write the final equation $y = A\sin\!\bigl(B(x - C)\bigr) + D$ with your values from (a)–(d). Verify by substituting $x = \tfrac{\pi}{6}$ and confirming $y = 7$. [3]
PART III  ·  MODELING / APPLIEDUniversal sinusoidal modelling · 28 marks

Section C · Modeling and Applications

Name your variables (with units) before writing equations. For each model, identify $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$ from the situation, then write the function and answer the contextual question. Calculator permitted throughout Part III, but exact-value answers in radical form are preferred where reasonable.

Q10MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ Honors / Pre-Calc §7 Ferris-Wheel Model · HSF-TF.B.5 [9 marks]

A Ferris wheel has a diameter of $40$ m. Its lowest point is $2$ m above the ground (the boarding platform), and it completes one full rotation every $80$ seconds. Riders board at the lowest point at time $t = 0$ and the wheel rotates at a constant rate. Let $h(t)$ be the height of a rider above the ground, in metres, $t$ seconds after boarding.

(a) State the centre height (midline $D$), the amplitude $A$, and the period $T$ of $h(t)$. [3]
(b) Write $h(t)$ in the form $h(t) = -A\cos\!\bigl(B t\bigr) + D$ (the negative cosine form starts at the minimum at $t = 0$, matching the boarding condition). State $B$ exactly. [2]
(c) Find the height of the rider $30$ seconds after boarding. Round to the nearest tenth of a metre. [2]
(d) At what time during the first rotation does the rider first reach a height of $30$ m on the way up? Round to the nearest second. [2]
Q11MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style Honors / Pre-Calc §7 Tidal Model · MHF4U Strand B [9 marks]

A coastal harbour records a high tide of $4.8$ m and a low tide of $0.6$ m. Successive high tides occur $12$ hours apart. The first high tide of the day occurs at $t = 3$ hours (where $t$ is hours after midnight). Let $H(t)$ be the water depth at the harbour at time $t$, in metres.

(a) Compute the midline $D$, the amplitude $A$, and the period $T$ of $H(t)$. [3]
(b) The cosine function naturally peaks at its argument $= 0$. Write $H(t)$ in the form $H(t) = A\cos\!\bigl(B(t - C)\bigr) + D$ using the high-tide time as the phase shift $C$. State $B$ and $C$ explicitly. [3]
(c) Compute the water depth at $t = 7$ hours (i.e. 7 a.m.). Round to two decimal places. [2]
(d) A small ferry requires a minimum water depth of $2.0$ m to dock safely. Without solving, explain whether the ferry can dock at the time computed in part (c). [1]
Q12HARDHonors 🇨🇦 BC 🇨🇦 AB BC Provincial-style §7 Temperature Model · BC PC 12 / AB Math 30-1 GO 4.9 [10 marks]

The mean daily temperature in a Northern Alberta town, in degrees Celsius, is modelled by a sinusoidal function of the day of the year $d$ (where $d = 1$ on January 1 and $d = 365$ on December 31). Records show the warmest mean daily temperature of $24^{\circ}\text{C}$ occurs on day $d = 200$, and the coldest of $-22^{\circ}\text{C}$ occurs roughly half a year later. Let $T(d)$ denote the modelled temperature.

(a) State the midline $D$, the amplitude $A$, and the period (in days). [3]
(b) Determine $B$ exactly using $B = \dfrac{2\pi}{T_{\text{period}}}$. [1]
(c) Write $T(d) = A\cos\!\bigl(B(d - C)\bigr) + D$ using the maximum-day as the phase shift $C$. State the explicit equation. [2]
(d) Compute the modelled temperature on April 30 ($d = 120$). Round to the nearest degree. [2]
(e) Without solving algebraically, identify two days of the year (one in spring, one in fall) on which the modelled temperature equals the midline $D$. Justify briefly using the geometry of the cosine curve. [2]
