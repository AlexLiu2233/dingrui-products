Q1EASY 🇺🇸 US SAT-style MCQ §1 Recursive vs Explicit · HSF-IF.A.3 [3 marks]

A sequence is defined recursively by $a_1 = 4$ and $a_{n+1} = a_n + 6$. Which of the following is the explicit formula for $a_n$?

  1. (A) $a_n = 6n + 4$
  2. (B) $a_n = 4n + 6$
  3. (C) $a_n = 6n - 2$
  4. (D) $a_n = 4 \cdot 6^{n-1}$

  1. (A) $89$

In an arithmetic sequence, the $4$th term is $t_4 = 11$ and the $11$th term is $t_{11} = 46$.

(a) Find the common difference $d$. [2]
(b) Find the first term $a$ and write the explicit formula $t_n = a + (n - 1) d$. [2]
(c) Determine which term of the sequence equals $156$. [2]
Q5MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §5 Finite Geometric Sum · BC PC12 geometric series [7 marks]

Consider the geometric series $3 + 12 + 48 + 192 + \dots$

(a) State $a_1$ and the common ratio $r$. [1]
(b) Write the explicit formula for the $n$-th term $a_n$. [1]
(c) Use $S_n = a_1 (r^n - 1) / (r - 1)$ to compute the sum of the first $7$ terms. Show each substitution. [3]
(d) Briefly justify why the formula requires $r \ne 1$. [2]
PART II  ·  EXTENDED RESPONSEAP-feeder FRQ + honors · 36 marks

Section B · Extended Response

Show every algebraic step. State the type of sequence (arithmetic or geometric) and the parameters $(a_1, d)$ or $(a_1, r)$ before writing any sum formula. For infinite geometric problems, write $|r| < 1$ before applying $S_\infty = a_1 / (1 - r)$. No calculator on Q6–Q9 unless noted.

Q6MEDIUM 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §3 Arithmetic Series (Gauss) · HSF-BF.A.2 [8 marks]

A theatre has rows of seats arranged so that the front row has $18$ seats, and each subsequent row has $3$ more seats than the row in front of it. The theatre has $20$ rows.

(a) Identify $a_1$ and $d$, and write the explicit formula $a_n = a_1 + (n - 1) d$ for the number of seats in row $n$. [2]
(b) Determine the number of seats in the back row (row $20$). [2]
(c) Use the average-times-count form $S_n = \tfrac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n)$ to compute the total number of seats in the theatre. [2]
(d) Cross-check your answer using the parameter form $S_n = \tfrac{n}{2}(2 a_1 + (n - 1) d)$. Comment briefly on the equivalence of the two forms (Gauss's pairing argument). [2]
Q7MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §3 Arithmetic Series · MCR3U C2.2 [8 marks]

Consider the arithmetic series $7 + 11 + 15 + 19 + \dots + 247$.

(a) State $a_1$ and $d$. [1]
(b) Determine the number of terms $n$ in the series. [3]
(c) Compute the sum of the series using $S_n = \tfrac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n)$. [2]
(d) State, using function notation, the value of $S(10) - S(5)$ where $S(k)$ denotes the partial sum of the first $k$ terms. Interpret this in one sentence. [2]
Q8HARDHonors 🇨🇦 BC 🇺🇸 US AP-feeder FRQ §6 Infinite Geometric · BC PC12 / HSA-SSE.B.4 [10 marks]

Consider the infinite geometric series with first term $a_1 = 18$ and an unknown common ratio $r$.

(a) State the convergence test for an infinite geometric series, citing the condition on $|r|$ explicitly. [1]
(b) Suppose $r = 2/3$. Show that $|r| < 1$, then compute $S_\infty = a_1 / (1 - r)$. [2]
(c) Suppose instead that $S_\infty = 24$. Find the value of $r$. [3]
(d) Express the repeating decimal $0.\overline{45} = 0.454545\ldots$ as an infinite geometric series, state $a_1$ and $r$, verify $|r| < 1$, and use $S_\infty = a_1/(1-r)$ to write the decimal as a fraction in lowest terms. [4]
Q9HARDHonors 🇺🇸 US 🇨🇦 BC AP-feeder FRQ §7 Sigma Notation · HSA-SSE.B.4 / BC PC12 [10 marks]

Sigma notation packages a series compactly. For each part, identify whether the sum is arithmetic or geometric, then evaluate it in closed form.

(a) Evaluate $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{30} (2k + 5)$. Identify $a_1$, $a_{30}$, and $d$, then apply the arithmetic sum formula. [3]
(b) Evaluate $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{6} 4 \cdot 3^{k-1}$. Identify $a_1$ and $r$, then apply the finite geometric sum formula. [3]
(c) Evaluate the infinite sum $\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} 5 \cdot \left(\tfrac{1}{4}\right)^{k}$. First verify that $|r| < 1$, then apply $S_\infty = a_1/(1 - r)$. [2]
(d) Pull out the constant and split the sum: rewrite $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{10} (7 \cdot 2^{k-1} - 3)$ as $7 \cdot \sum_{k=1}^{10} 2^{k-1} - \sum_{k=1}^{10} 3$, evaluate each piece, and state the total. [2]

Q10MEDIUM 🇨🇦 ON ON Provincial-style §4 Compound Interest · MCR3U C3.1 [10 marks]

Sara deposits CA$2000 into a savings account that earns $4\%$ interest compounded annually. Let $A_n$ denote the balance, in dollars, at the end of year $n$, with $A_0 = 2000$ being the initial deposit.

(a) Argue why $(A_n)$ is a geometric sequence and state $a_1 = A_1$ and the common ratio $r$. [2]
(b) Write the explicit formula $A_n = 2000 \cdot (1.04)^{n}$ and use it to compute the balance after $10$ years. [3]
(c) After how many full years does the balance first exceed CA$3000? Show the inequality you solve. [3]
(d) Briefly state, in one sentence, the connection between this geometric sequence and the exponential function $f(t) = 2000 \cdot (1.04)^{t}$ (Ontario MCR3U C3.1; BC PC12 names the same connection). [2]
Q11MEDIUM 🇨🇦 BC BC Provincial-style §4 Geometric Decay · BC PC12 exponential ↔ geometric [10 marks]

A medical isotope decays in such a way that each day the amount remaining is $85\%$ of the amount that was present at the start of the previous day. A patient is administered a $40$ mg dose at the start of day $1$. Let $a_n$ denote the amount (in mg) remaining at the start of day $n$, so $a_1 = 40$.

(a) Argue why $(a_n)$ is a geometric sequence and state the common ratio $r$. [2]
(b) Write the explicit formula $a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}$ and use it to compute the amount remaining at the start of day $8$. [2]
(c) The patient must wait until the amount remaining first drops below $5$ mg before a second dose is given. Solve $a_n < 5$ for the smallest integer $n$. [3]
(d) Describe in one sentence how this geometric sequence is a sample of a continuous exponential-decay function (BC PC12 Curricular Competency: exponential functions to geometric sequences). [2]
(e) State one constraint on $n$ that arises from the situation. [1]
Q12HARD 🇨🇦 BC 🇺🇸 US BC Provincial-style §5 Finite Geometric Sum · HSA-SSE.B.4 [12 marks]

An employee accepts a contract that pays CA$30000 in the first year, with each subsequent year's salary increasing by exactly $5\%$ over the previous year. Let $a_n$ denote the salary, in dollars, in year $n$.

(a) Argue why $(a_n)$ is a geometric sequence and state $a_1$ and $r$. [2]
(b) Write the explicit formula $a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n - 1}$ and use it to compute the salary in year $10$. [2]
(c) Use the finite geometric sum $S_n = a_1 (r^n - 1) / (r - 1)$ to compute the total amount earned over the first $10$ years. [3]
(d) Determine the smallest integer $n$ for which the cumulative total $S_n$ first exceeds CA$500000. Show the inequality you solve. [3]
(e) Briefly compare with the alternative offer of a fixed CA$42000 per year (an arithmetic series with $d = 0$): over the first $10$ years, which offer pays more in total? State the comparison explicitly. [2]
