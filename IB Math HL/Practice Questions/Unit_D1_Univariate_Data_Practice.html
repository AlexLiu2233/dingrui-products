Q1EASY Paper 1A 4.1 Sampling [5 marks]

A national exam board has $40\,000$ candidates split across $50$ schools. To check whether grade boundaries are fair, the board chooses a sample of $400$ scripts by first randomly selecting $5$ schools, then taking every $10\text{th}$ script from each chosen school's alphabetical roster.

(a) Name the two sampling techniques combined in this design. [2]
(b) Give one source of bias this scheme could still introduce. [1]
(c) Classify the variable "exam score, out of 80" as discrete or continuous, with a one-line reason. [2]
Q2EASY Paper 1A 4.3 Central Tendency [5 marks]

The number of goals scored by a striker in each of her last $9$ league matches was:

$1, \quad 2, \quad 0, \quad 3, \quad 2, \quad 5, \quad 1, \quad 0, \quad 4$

(a) Find the mean number of goals per match. [2]
(b) Find the median. [2]
(c) State the mode. [1]
Q3MEDIUM Paper 1A 4.2 IQR & Outliers [6 marks]

A sorted dataset of $11$ values has $Q_1 = 14$, median $= 22$, and $Q_3 = 30$. The largest value in the set is $54$ and the smallest is $4$.

(a) Compute the interquartile range $\text{IQR}$. [1]
(b) Using the $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ rule, determine the upper and lower fence values. [2]
(c) State, with reasons, which (if any) of $4$ and $54$ should be flagged as outliers. [3]
Q4MEDIUM Paper 1A 4.3 Linear Transformations [6 marks]

A dataset of student marks $\{x_i\}$ has mean $\bar{x} = 58$ and standard deviation $\sigma_x = 12$. The teacher rescales every mark by $y_i = 1.25 x_i - 5$ to align with a different rubric.

(a) Find the mean $\bar{y}$ of the rescaled marks. [2]
(b) Find the standard deviation $\sigma_y$ of the rescaled marks. [2]
(c) A second teacher applies the further transformation $z_i = -y_i + 90$ ("90 minus") to the rescaled marks. Find $\bar{z}$ and $\sigma_z$. [2]

Q7HARD Paper 1B 4.2 / 4.3 Outliers & Transformations [6 marks]

A dataset of $10$ test scores has $\bar{x} = 64$, $\sigma_x = 8$, $Q_1 = 60$, median $= 65$, $Q_3 = 70$.

After grading, the teacher discovers that one student's mark was misread: a score of $40$ was actually $80$. The teacher corrects the dataset (one value changes from $40$ to $80$).

(a) Under the $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ rule, would $40$ have been flagged as an outlier in the original dataset? Justify. [2]
(b) The teacher then rescales the corrected scores by $y_i = 1.1 x_i - 4$. Show that the new mean increases by exactly $4$ marks. (Hint: compare $\bar{y}$ to $\bar{x}_{\text{corrected}}$.) [2]
(c) Explain why $\sigma_x$ alone could not have told the teacher whether the misread $40$ was an outlier. [2]
