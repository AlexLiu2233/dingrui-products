Q1EASYPaper 1A4.1 Sampling[5 marks]

National exam board sampling: 5 schools randomly, every 10th script. (a) Name techniques. (b) One bias source. (c) Discrete or continuous?国家考试委员会抽样：随机抽 5 所学校，每第 10 份试卷。(a) 写出抽样方法名称。(b) 一种偏差来源。(c) 离散还是连续？

Answers:答案：  (a) cluster (schools) + systematic (every 10th)整群抽样（学校）+ 系统抽样（每第 10 份）  ·  (b) within-cluster homogeneity (or roster-ordering bias)群内同质性（或名单顺序偏差）  ·  (c) discrete离散

(a) Two sampling techniques A1·A1

  • Cluster sampling — the 50 schools form natural clusters, and 5 are chosen at random. A whole-cluster-level selection rule.
  • Systematic sampling — within each chosen school, every $10\text{th}$ script from the alphabetical roster. A fixed-step rule along an ordered list.

(b) One source of bias R1

Any one of:
  • Within-cluster homogeneity: students at the same school tend to have similar background / teacher / preparation, so the 5 selected schools may not be representative of the full 50.
  • Roster-ordering bias: if the alphabetical roster correlates with anything systematic (e.g. surnames clustered by ethnicity or family background), every-$10\text{th}$ could over-represent certain groups.
Award the mark for either, with reasoning. Do not accept "selection bias" alone — IB wants the mechanism.

(c) Discrete vs. continuous A1·R1

The variable "exam score, out of 80" is discrete. Reason: scores take integer values $0, 1, 2, \ldots, 80$ — a countable set with no values in between (e.g. $47.3$ is not possible).
IB convention. "Discrete" requires the values to be countable; "continuous" requires the variable to be measurable on a real interval. Exam marks, counts, ratings $1$–$5$ → discrete. Time, length, mass → continuous (in principle; rounding to nearest minute / cm is still treated as continuous for grouped-data work).

(a) 两种抽样方法 A1·A1

  • 整群抽样（cluster sampling） —— $50$ 所学校构成天然的"群"，从中随机抽取 $5$ 个。属于"整群级别"的选取规则。
  • 系统抽样（systematic sampling） —— 在每所被选学校的字母顺序名单中每隔 $10$ 份取 $1$ 份。是在有序列表上以固定步长进行选取的规则。

(b) 一种偏差来源 R1

下列任一即可：
  • 群内同质性。同校学生在背景、师资、备考方式上往往相近，因此被抽到的 $5$ 所学校未必能代表全部 $50$ 所。
  • 名单顺序偏差。若字母顺序的名单与某种系统性因素（例如姓氏与族群或家庭背景相关）相关，则每第 $10$ 份的取法可能过度代表某些群体。
上述任一只要说明机制都可得分。不接受仅写"selection bias"（选择偏差）—— IB 要的是具体机制。

(c) 离散还是连续 A1·R1

变量"满分 80 的考试成绩"是离散型（discrete）。理由：分数取整数值 $0, 1, 2, \ldots, 80$ —— 这是一个可数集合，两个整数之间没有可能取值（例如不可能出现 $47.3$）。
IB 惯例。"离散（discrete）"要求取值可数；"连续（continuous）"要求变量在某个实数区间上可测量。考试分数、计数、$1$–$5$ 评分 → 离散。时间、长度、质量 → 连续（原则上如此；即便四舍五入到最近的分钟或厘米，在分组数据处理中仍视为连续）。
Q2EASYPaper 1A4.3 Central Tendency[5 marks]

Goals scored: $2, 1, 0, 3, 2, 1, 2, 4, 3$ ($n = 9$). (a) Mean. (b) Median. (c) Mode.进球数：$2, 1, 0, 3, 2, 1, 2, 4, 3$（$n = 9$）。(a) 均值。(b) 中位数。(c) 众数。

Answers:答案：  (a) $\bar{x} = 2$  ·  (b) median $= 2$中位数 $= 2$  ·  (c) mode $= 2$众数 $= 2$

(a) Mean M1·A1

$$ \bar{x} \;=\; \frac{2 + 1 + 0 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 2 + 4 + 3}{9} \;=\; \frac{18}{9} \;=\; 2. $$

(b) Median M1·A1

Sort in ascending order: $0, 1, 1, 2, \boxed{2}, 2, 3, 3, 4$. With $n = 9$, the median is the $\left(\dfrac{n+1}{2}\right) = 5\text{th}$ value, which is $\boxed{2}$.

(c) Mode A1

Frequency tally: $0\!\to\!1,\ 1\!\to\!2,\ 2\!\to\!3,\ 3\!\to\!2,\ 4\!\to\!1$. The value $2$ appears three times (more than any other), so the mode is $\boxed{2}$.
When all three coincide. A symmetric, single-peaked dataset often has mean $=$ median $=$ mode. Here all three equal $2$, which is consistent with a roughly symmetric distribution. Don't expect this in general — for skewed data the mean and median diverge.

(a) 均值（mean） M1·A1

$$ \bar{x} \;=\; \frac{2 + 1 + 0 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 2 + 4 + 3}{9} \;=\; \frac{18}{9} \;=\; 2. $$

(b) 中位数（median） M1·A1

升序排列：$0, 1, 1, 2, \boxed{2}, 2, 3, 3, 4$。当 $n = 9$ 时，中位数取第 $\left(\dfrac{n+1}{2}\right) = 5$ 个值，即 $\boxed{2}$。

(c) 众数（mode） A1

频数统计：$0\!\to\!1,\ 1\!\to\!2,\ 2\!\to\!3,\ 3\!\to\!2,\ 4\!\to\!1$。值 $2$ 出现 $3$ 次（多于其他任何值），故众数为 $\boxed{2}$。
三者重合的情形。对称且单峰的数据集往往满足 均值 $=$ 中位数 $=$ 众数。本题三者均为 $2$，与近似对称的分布吻合。但不可一概而论 —— 偏态数据中均值与中位数会分开。
Q3MEDIUMPaper 1A4.2 IQR & Outliers[6 marks]

$Q_1 = 14$, median $= 22$, $Q_3 = 30$. Smallest value $= 4$, largest $= 54$. (a) IQR. (b) Fence values. (c) Outlier flags?$Q_1 = 14$、中位数 $= 22$、$Q_3 = 30$。最小值 $= 4$，最大值 $= 54$。(a) IQR。(b) 围栏值。(c) 是否标记为异常值？

Answers:答案：  (a) $\text{IQR} = 16$  ·  (b) lower fence $= -10$, upper fence $= 54$下围栏 $= -10$，上围栏 $= 54$  ·  (c) neither flagged两者均未标记

(a) IQR A1

$\text{IQR} \;=\; Q_3 - Q_1 \;=\; 30 - 14 \;=\; \boxed{16}.$

(b) Fence values M1·A1

Apply $1.5\cdot\text{IQR} = 24$ above $Q_3$ and below $Q_1$: $$ \text{Lower fence} \;=\; Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR} \;=\; 14 - 24 \;=\; -10, $$ $$ \text{Upper fence} \;=\; Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR} \;=\; 30 + 24 \;=\; 54. $$

(c) Outlier check M1·A1·R1

  • Smallest value $4$: $-10 \le 4 \le 14$ (within $[\text{lower fence},\, Q_1]$), so $4$ is not beyond the lower fence — not an outlier.
  • Largest value $54$: equals the upper fence exactly. The $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ rule flags values strictly beyond a fence (typically written $x > Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR}$, strict inequality), so a value sitting on the fence is not flagged. Mention the strict-inequality convention to secure the reasoning mark.
Boundary case. Some sources use $\ge$ instead of $>$ for the outlier cutoff, in which case $54$ would be flagged. For IB AA HL the convention is strict inequality on both sides ($x < Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR}$ or $x > Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR}$). Either answer can earn the marks if the convention is stated.

(a) IQR A1

四分位距（IQR）$= Q_3 - Q_1 = 30 - 14 = \boxed{16}$。

(b) 围栏值 M1·A1

在 $Q_1$ 之下和 $Q_3$ 之上各取 $1.5\cdot\text{IQR} = 24$： $$ \text{下围栏（lower fence）} \;=\; Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR} \;=\; 14 - 24 \;=\; -10, $$ $$ \text{上围栏（upper fence）} \;=\; Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR} \;=\; 30 + 24 \;=\; 54. $$

(c) 异常值判定 M1·A1·R1

  • 最小值 $4$：$-10 \le 4 \le 14$（处于 $[\text{下围栏},\, Q_1]$ 之中），因此 $4$ 没有越过下围栏 —— 不是异常值。
  • 最大值 $54$：恰好等于上围栏。$1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ 规则只把严格越出围栏的值标记为异常（通常写作 $x > Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR}$，严格不等式），所以恰在围栏上的值不被标记。要把"严格不等式"这一惯例写出来才能拿到推理分。
边界情形。部分教材把异常值的临界用 $\ge$ 而非 $>$，那样 $54$ 就会被标记。IB AA HL 的惯例是两边都用严格不等式（$x < Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR}$ 或 $x > Q_3 + 1.5\,\text{IQR}$）。只要明写所用惯例，两种答案都能得分。
Q4MEDIUMPaper 1A4.3 Linear Transformations[6 marks]

$\bar{x} = 58$, $\sigma_x = 12$. Apply $y_i = 1.25 x_i - 5$, then $z_i = -y_i + 90$.$\bar{x} = 58$、$\sigma_x = 12$。先施加 $y_i = 1.25 x_i - 5$，再施加 $z_i = -y_i + 90$。

Answers:答案：  (a) $\bar{y} = 67.5$  ·  (b) $\sigma_y = 15$  ·  (c) $\bar{z} = 22.5$, $\sigma_z = 15$

Lemma to use R1

For any linear transformation $y_i = a x_i + b$: $$ \bar{y} \;=\; a\bar{x} + b, \qquad \sigma_y \;=\; |a|\,\sigma_x. $$ The mean shifts by both $a$ and $b$; the SD scales by $|a|$ only (and is therefore invariant under a pure shift $b$).

(a) Rescaled mean M1·A1

$$ \bar{y} \;=\; 1.25 \cdot 58 - 5 \;=\; 72.5 - 5 \;=\; \boxed{67.5}. $$

(b) Rescaled SD M1·A1

$$ \sigma_y \;=\; |1.25| \cdot 12 \;=\; \boxed{15}. $$

(c) Second transformation $z_i = -y_i + 90$ M1·A1

Here $a = -1$, $b = 90$ applied to $y_i$: $$ \bar{z} \;=\; -\bar{y} + 90 \;=\; -67.5 + 90 \;=\; \boxed{22.5}, $$ $$ \sigma_z \;=\; |-1| \cdot \sigma_y \;=\; 1 \cdot 15 \;=\; \boxed{15}. $$
Why $|a|$ and not $a$. Standard deviation is a measure of spread; spread can't be negative. Multiplying every value by $-1$ reflects the dataset about $0$ but doesn't change its dispersion. The mean does flip sign because mean is a location measure.

需要用到的引理 R1

对任意线性变换（linear transformation）$y_i = a x_i + b$： $$ \bar{y} \;=\; a\bar{x} + b, \qquad \sigma_y \;=\; |a|\,\sigma_x. $$ 均值同时受 $a$ 与 $b$ 影响；标准差只按 $|a|$ 缩放，因此对纯位移 $b$ 不变。

(a) 变换后的均值 M1·A1

$$ \bar{y} \;=\; 1.25 \cdot 58 - 5 \;=\; 72.5 - 5 \;=\; \boxed{67.5}. $$

(b) 变换后的标准差 M1·A1

$$ \sigma_y \;=\; |1.25| \cdot 12 \;=\; \boxed{15}. $$

(c) 第二次变换 $z_i = -y_i + 90$ M1·A1

对 $y_i$ 而言此时 $a = -1$、$b = 90$： $$ \bar{z} \;=\; -\bar{y} + 90 \;=\; -67.5 + 90 \;=\; \boxed{22.5}, $$ $$ \sigma_z \;=\; |-1| \cdot \sigma_y \;=\; 1 \cdot 15 \;=\; \boxed{15}. $$
为何取 $|a|$ 而非 $a$。标准差度量离散程度，不可能为负。把所有数值乘以 $-1$ 只是把数据关于 $0$ 翻转，并不改变其离散程度。但均值会变号 —— 因为均值是一个位置（location）度量。

Q7HARDPaper 1B4.2 / 4.3 Outliers & Transformations[6 marks]

$n=10$, $\bar{x}=64$, $\sigma_x=8$, $Q_1=60$, median $=65$, $Q_3=70$. One value $40$ corrected to $80$. Then $y_i = 1.1 x_i - 4$.$n=10$、$\bar{x}=64$、$\sigma_x=8$、$Q_1=60$、中位数 $=65$、$Q_3=70$。其中一个 $40$ 改为 $80$。随后 $y_i = 1.1 x_i - 4$。

Answers:答案：  (a) yes ($40 < 45 = $ lower fence)是（$40 < 45 = $ 下围栏）  ·  (b) $\bar{y} = 70.8$; $2.8$ above $\bar{x}_{\text{corr}} = 68$$\bar{y} = 70.8$；比 $\bar{x}_{\text{corr}} = 68$ 高 $2.8$  ·  (c) SD measures spread, not extremeness标准差度量离散程度，而非极端程度

(a) Was $40$ an outlier in the original dataset? M1·A1

$\text{IQR} = 70 - 60 = 10$; $1.5\,\text{IQR} = 15$. Lower fence $= Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR} = 60 - 15 = 45$. Since $40 < 45$, the value $40$ is below the lower fence — flagged as an outlier under the $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ rule. ✓

(b) Corrected mean and rescaled mean M1·A1

Correcting one value from $40$ to $80$ adds $40$ to the total. Original total $= 64 \cdot 10 = 640$; corrected total $= 680$; corrected mean $$ \bar{x}_{\text{corr}} \;=\; \frac{680}{10} \;=\; 68. $$ Now apply $y_i = 1.1 x_i - 4$: $$ \bar{y} \;=\; 1.1 \cdot 68 - 4 \;=\; 74.8 - 4 \;=\; \boxed{70.8}. $$ The rescaled mean sits $70.8 - 68 = \boxed{2.8}$ marks above the corrected mean.

(c) SD vs. outlier-detection R1·R1

The SD $\sigma_x = 8$ quantifies the typical spread around the mean, but it doesn't directly tell you which individual values are extreme. The $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ rule operates on quartiles, which are positional (where do values land?) rather than central-moment-based. A point can be many SDs from the mean without being beyond a fence, and vice versa, depending on shape. So $\sigma_x$ alone cannot identify which value (if any) is an outlier; you need $Q_1$ and $Q_3$ for that.
Two outlier philosophies. IB defaults to the $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ rule (positional, robust to skew). Many real-world statisticians use the $2\sigma$ or $3\sigma$ rule (moment-based, valid under approximate normality). They give different answers on skewed data; the IB rule is preferred for AA HL.

(a) 原始数据集中的 $40$ 是否为异常值？ M1·A1

$\text{IQR} = 70 - 60 = 10$；$1.5\,\text{IQR} = 15$。下围栏 $= Q_1 - 1.5\,\text{IQR} = 60 - 15 = 45$。由于 $40 < 45$，$40$ 确实在下围栏之外 —— 按 $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ 规则被标记为异常值。✓

(b) 改正后的均值与变换后的均值 M1·A1

把一个 $40$ 改为 $80$，等于总和增加 $40$。原始总和 $= 64 \cdot 10 = 640$；改正后总和 $= 680$；改正后均值 $$ \bar{x}_{\text{corr}} \;=\; \frac{680}{10} \;=\; 68. $$ 再施加 $y_i = 1.1 x_i - 4$： $$ \bar{y} \;=\; 1.1 \cdot 68 - 4 \;=\; 74.8 - 4 \;=\; \boxed{70.8}. $$ 变换后的均值比改正后的均值高 $70.8 - 68 = \boxed{2.8}$ 分。

(c) 标准差与异常值检测 R1·R1

$\sigma_x = 8$ 度量数据围绕均值的典型波动幅度，但并不直接告诉你哪些个别值是极端的。$1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ 规则建立在四分位数（quartiles）上，这是一种位置型指标（数据落在何处？），而非围绕中心的矩。一个点可以离均值多个标准差但并未越过围栏，反之亦然，具体取决于分布形状。因此仅凭 $\sigma_x$ 无法识别出哪个值（如果有）是异常值；为此你需要 $Q_1$ 与 $Q_3$。
两种异常值判定哲学。IB 默认采用 $1.5\cdot\text{IQR}$ 规则（位置型，对偏态稳健）。许多实际工作中的统计学家使用 $2\sigma$ 或 $3\sigma$ 规则（矩型，仅在近似正态时成立）。在偏态数据上两者给出的答案会不同；AA HL 偏好 IB 规则。

(d) Why standardising erases the difference R1

$z$-scores erase scale. That's the point: $z$-scores let you compare values across datasets with different units / scales (test scores vs heights, kg vs tons). The trade-off is exactly what the question highlights — you also erase information about absolute spread.

标准化把每个 $x_i$ 替换为 $z_i = (x_i - \bar{x})/\sigma_x$。根据构造，标准化后的数据必满足 $\bar{z} = 0$、$\sigma_z = 1$，与原标准差无关。因此标准化之后，两组的离散程度都变成 $\sigma_z = 1$ —— 原本的离散程度差异恰恰就是我们除掉的那一项。
