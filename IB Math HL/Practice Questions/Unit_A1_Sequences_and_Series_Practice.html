Q1EASY Paper 1A 1.2 Arithmetic Sequences [5 marks]

An arithmetic sequence has $u_5 = 23$ and $u_{15} = 53$.

(a) Find the common difference $d$. [2]
(b) Find $u_1$. [2]
(c) Find $S_{10}$. [1]
Q2EASY Paper 1A 1.3 Geometric Sequences [4 marks]

A geometric sequence has $u_2 = 12$ and $u_5 = -96$.

(a) Find the common ratio $r$. [2]
(b) Find $u_1$ and $u_8$. [2]
Q3MEDIUM Paper 1A 1.4 Sigma Notation [5 marks]

Evaluate $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{20}(4k - 3)$ by recognising the summand as an arithmetic sequence and applying the appropriate sum formula. Show every step.

Q4MEDIUM Paper 1A 1.8 Infinite GP [6 marks]

Express $0.\overline{42} = 0.424242\ldots$ as a fraction in its simplest form. You must justify your answer using the formula for the sum of an infinite geometric series, including a check of the convergence condition.

Q5MEDIUM Paper 1A 1.8 Infinite GP [6 marks]

An infinite geometric series has first term $u_1 = 18$ and sum to infinity $S_\infty = 24$.

(a) Find the common ratio $r$, expressing your answer as an exact fraction. [3]
(b) Find $S_5$ (the sum of the first five terms), expressing your answer as an exact fraction. [3]
Q6MEDIUM Paper 1A 1.2 / 1.3 Mixed [5 marks]

The first three terms of a sequence are $4,\; x,\; 9$.

(a) If the sequence is arithmetic, find $x$. [1]
(b) If the sequence is geometric, find all possible values of $x$. [2]
(c) Hence determine, with justification, whether there exists a value of $x$ for which the sequence $4, x, 9$ is both arithmetic and geometric. [2]

Q7HARD Paper 1B 1.2 / 1.4 Multi-skill [16 marks]

The sum of the first $n$ terms of a sequence $\{u_n\}$ is given by

$$ S_n = n^2 + 3n. $$
(a) Find $u_1$, $u_2$, and $u_3$ directly from the definition $u_n = S_n - S_{n-1}$ for $n \ge 2$, and $u_1 = S_1$. [3]
(b) Show that for $n \ge 2$, $u_n = 2n + 2$. [3]
(c) Verify that the formula in part (b) also gives the correct value of $u_1$. [1]
(d) Hence show that $\{u_n\}$ is an arithmetic sequence, stating its common difference. [2]
(e) Find a closed-form expression in $n$ for $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{n} u_{2k}$ (the sum of the first $n$ even-indexed terms). [4]
(f) Hence evaluate $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{15} u_{2k}$. [3]

Q8MEDIUM Paper 2 1.4 Compound Interest [8 marks]

On 1 January 2025, Lily invests €$5000$ in an account paying $4.2\%$ per annum compounded quarterly.

(a) Find, to the nearest cent, the value of Lily's investment on 1 January 2031. [2]
(b) Lily wants the value to first exceed €$7500$. Find the year in which this first occurs (i.e. the first 1 January date on which the balance exceeds €$7500$). [3]
(c) On the same date, Jenny invests €$5000$ at $4.2\%$ compounded annually instead. Find the difference, to the nearest cent, between Lily's and Jenny's account values on 1 January 2031, and state in one sentence why Lily's value is the larger of the two. [3]
Q9MEDIUM Paper 2 1.4 / 1.8 Real-World GP [7 marks]

A ball is dropped from a height of $10~\mathrm{m}$. After each bounce, it rises to $75\%$ of the previous height. Air resistance is negligible.

(a) Find the height of the third bounce, in metres. [2]
(b) Find the total vertical distance the ball travels before coming to rest, giving your answer in metres. [4]
(c) State, in one sentence, why this total distance is finite even though the ball bounces infinitely many times. [1]
Q10HARD Paper 2 1.2 / 1.3 / 1.4 Hybrid [9 marks]

A worker's annual salary in year $n$ forms an arithmetic sequence $\{u_n\}$. The year-1 salary is €$42\,000$ and the year-30 salary is €$71\,000$.

(a) Find the annual increment $d$. [2]
(b) Find the worker's total earnings over the 30-year career. [3]
(c) Suppose instead the worker's annual salary $\{v_n\}$ formed a geometric sequence with the same year-1 and year-30 salaries.
(i) Find the common ratio $r$, correct to four decimal places. [2]
(ii) Without computing the GP total in full, state with justification whether the geometric scheme produces higher or lower total earnings than the arithmetic scheme. [2]

Q11HARD Paper 3 1.8 Infinite GP & Power Series [16 marks]

This question explores the link between the infinite geometric series and certain related power-series identities.

(a) State the formula for the sum to infinity $S_\infty$ of a convergent infinite geometric series with first term $u_1$ and common ratio $r$, and give the exact convergence condition on $r$. [2]
(b) Hence write down, for $|x| < 1$, the closed-form value of $$ f(x) = 1 + x + x^2 + x^3 + x^4 + \cdots $$ [1]
(c) By differentiating both sides of the identity in (b) term-by-term (treating $f$ as a function of $x$), derive a closed-form expression for $$ g(x) = 1 + 2x + 3x^2 + 4x^3 + 5x^4 + \cdots, \qquad |x| < 1. $$ Show every step of the differentiation. [4]
(d) Use your closed-form expression for $g(x)$ to compute the exact value of $$ \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} k\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{\!k-1}. $$ [3]
(e) Verify your answer to (d) numerically by computing the partial sum $\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{10} k\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)^{\!k-1}$ on your GDC. State the value to 4 decimal places and comment briefly on its proximity to the predicted limit. [2]
(f) The triangular numbers are $T_n = \tfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$, giving the sequence $1, 3, 6, 10, 15, \ldots$ Differentiate $g(x)$ term-by-term and hence conjecture (without rigorous proof) a closed-form expression for $$ h(x) = 1 + 3x + 6x^2 + 10x^3 + 15x^4 + \cdots, \qquad |x| < 1. $$ [4]
