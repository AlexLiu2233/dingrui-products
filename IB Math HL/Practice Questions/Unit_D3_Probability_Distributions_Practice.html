Q2EASY Paper 1A 4.7 Fair Game [5 marks]

A fair coin is tossed. If it lands heads, the player wins $\$5$; if tails, the player loses $\$3$.

(a) Find the expected profit per toss. [2]
(b) State whether the game is fair, and explain. [1]
(c) Find the win amount $\$w$ for heads (the loss for tails staying at $\$3$) that would make the game fair. [2]
Q3MEDIUM Paper 1A 4.8 Binomial Exact [6 marks]

A biased coin lands heads with probability $\dfrac{1}{3}$. It is tossed $3$ times. Let $X$ be the number of heads.

(a) Write out the probability distribution of $X$ in a table, as exact fractions over $27$. [3]
(b) Find $E(X)$ either from the table or by quoting $np$. [1]
(c) Find $P(X \ge 1)$. [2]
Q4MEDIUM Paper 1A 4.9 Normal (with tabulated $z$) [5 marks]

$X \sim N(50, 100)$. You are given $P(Z < 1) = 0.8413$, where $Z = \dfrac{X - \mu}{\sigma}$.

(a) Find the $z$-score corresponding to $x = 60$. [1]
(b) Hence find $P(X < 60)$. [1]
(c) Use symmetry of the standard normal to find $P(40 < X < 60)$. [3]

Q5MEDIUM Paper 1B 4.8 Binomial Cumulative [7 marks]

A field-hockey player has scoring rate $\dfrac{1}{2}$ per penalty stroke. She takes $5$ independent strokes. Let $X$ be the number of scores.

(a) Find $P(X = 2)$ as an exact fraction over $32$. [2]
(b) Find $P(X \le 1)$. [2]
(c) Find $P(X \ge 3)$. [2]

Q6HARD Paper 1B AHL 4.11 Variance [8 marks] HL

$x$$-1$$0$$1$$2$
$P(X = x)$$\dfrac{1}{8}$$\dfrac{3}{8}$$\dfrac{3}{8}$$\dfrac{1}{8}$

Q7HARD Paper 1B AHL 4.11 Continuous PDF [8 marks] HL

(b) Find $P(X > 1)$. [2]
(c) Find the median $m$ of $X$. [3]

Q11HARD Paper 3 AHL 4.11 Linear Transforms / Sums [15 marks] HL

Let $X \sim N(100,\, 15^2)$ and $Y \sim N(80,\, 20^2)$ be independent random variables.

(a) Find $E(2X + 3)$ and $\text{Var}(2X + 3)$. [2]
(b) Find $E(X + Y)$ and $\text{Var}(X + Y)$. [2]
(c) State the distribution of $X + Y$ and hence find $P(X + Y > 200)$, to 3 s.f. [3]
(d) Find $E(X - Y)$ and $\text{Var}(X - Y)$. Hence find $P(X > Y)$, to 3 s.f. [3]
(e) Find $c$ such that $P(X + Y > c) = 0.05$, to 1 d.p. [3]
(f) Explain, in one sentence, why $\text{Var}(X - Y) = \text{Var}(X + Y)$ even though $E(X - Y) \ne E(X + Y)$. [2]
