# British Columbia ADST — HS Computer Studies Extract (Grades 10-12)

**Source:** *BC's New Curriculum — Applied Design, Skills, and
Technologies (ADST)* (Province of British Columbia, Ministry of
Education). BC is **competency-based**: there are **no numeric standard
codes**. Each course is structured as **Big Ideas**, **Curricular
Competencies** (grouped under Applied Design / Applied Skills / Applied
Technologies), and **Content** (learning standards as bullet phrases).
Cite the **bullet text verbatim**, not a code.

**Courses captured** (the ADST grade 10-12 computer cluster relevant to
HS CS):
- **Computer Studies 10** — broad intro (hardware, software, networks,
  programming intro, society). Grade 10 floor.
- **Computer Programming 11** & **Computer Programming 12** — the
  programming-depth stream.
- **Computer Information Systems 11** & **Computer Information Systems
  12** — the systems/networks/ICT stream.

**Fetched via** the official curriculum site (the discipline pages
render server-side and were readable):
- <https://curriculum.gov.bc.ca/curriculum/adst/10/computer-studies>
- <https://curriculum.gov.bc.ca/curriculum/adst/11/computer-programming>

- <https://curriculum.gov.bc.ca/curriculum/adst/11/computer-information-systems>
- <https://curriculum.gov.bc.ca/curriculum/adst/12/computer-information-systems>

> **`honors-flag` guidance:** BC grade 12 (Computer Programming 12 /
> Computer Information Systems 12) is the harder stream over the grade 11
> floor. Grade-12-only content (advanced programming structures, pre-built
> data structures, network deployment/management, virtualization) carries
> the honors flag.

> **Language note (BC is language-flexible).** ADST does not mandate a
> programming language; Computer Programming 11/12 Content says
> "programming language constructs," "translation of design
> specifications into source code," "pre-built libraries." Use the
> pseudocode-first + Python-canonical pattern; `syllabus-note` only that
> BC leaves the language to the school.

## Curricular Competencies (shared design framework, verbatim — Computer Programming 11)

BC applies the **same Applied Design / Applied Skills / Applied
Technologies** competency framework across all ADST courses. These are
the process competencies a guide can cite for any unit (design, build,
test, share, ethics).

**Applied Design — Understanding context:**
> "Conduct user-centred research to understand design opportunities and
> barriers"

**Defining:**
> "Establish a point of view for a chosen design opportunity";
> "Identify potential users, intended impact, and possible unintended
> negative consequences"; "Make inferences about premises and
> constraints that define the design space"

**Ideating:**
> "Generate ideas and add to others' ideas to create possibilities, and
> prioritize them for prototyping"; "Critically analyze how competing
> social, ethical, and sustainability considerations impact designed
> solutions to meet global needs for preferred futures"; "Work with
> users throughout the design process"

**Prototyping / Testing / Making / Sharing (selected, verbatim):**
> "Construct prototypes, making changes to tools, materials, and
> procedures as needed"; "Develop an appropriate test of the prototype";
> "Use project management processes when working individually or
> collaboratively to coordinate production"; "Share progress while
> creating to increase opportunities for feedback".

**Applied Skills (verbatim):**
> "Apply safety procedures for themselves, co-workers, and users in both
> physical and digital environments"

**Applied Technologies (verbatim):**
> "Evaluate impacts, including unintended negative consequences, of
> choices made about technology use"; "Analyze the role technologies
> play in societal change"; "Examine how cultural beliefs, values, and
> ethical positions affect the development and use of technologies"

*(Computer Programming 12 carries the same competencies with minor
wording shifts, e.g. "Make decisions about premises and constraints"
in place of "Make inferences"; verify on the grade-12 page if quoting.)*

## Unit 5 — Data Structures (arrays, lists)

**Computer Programming 12 Content (verbatim, harder stream):**
> "Uses of pre-built data structures"

*Syllabus note:* BC names data structures only at the **grade-12** level
and only as "uses of pre-built data structures" — it does **not**
enumerate arrays/stacks/queues the way Ontario ICS4U or CSTA 3B-AP-12 do.
`honors-flag` + `syllabus-note` for a Data Structures guide.

---

## Unit 6 — Strings and Text Processing

**Computer Studies 10 Content (verbatim):**
> "…to store and manipulate numbers and text…" (within the
> "planning and writing simple programs" bullet)

*Syllabus note:* BC has **no dedicated string-processing learning
standard**. Text manipulation is implicit in the "store and manipulate
numbers and text" phrase in Computer Studies 10. Treat string content as
language-feature material with a BC `syllabus-note`.

---

## Unit 7 — Searching and Sorting

*Syllabus note:* BC's ADST computer courses have **no explicit
searching/sorting or algorithm-efficiency learning standard**.
The closest is Computer Programming 12 "Advanced programming structures"
and "Management of complexity" (verbatim). A Searching & Sorting guide
should `syllabus-note` that BC does not name these algorithms; they are
implied under advanced structures. Contrast: Ontario ICS4U and CSTA
3B-AP-10/-11 name searching/sorting explicitly.

---

## Unit 8 — Object-Oriented Programming (Intro)

**Computer Studies 10 Content (verbatim):**
> "introductory computer programming concepts and constructs (classes,
> objects, data types, …)"

*Syllabus note:* BC mentions "classes, objects" only in the Computer
Studies 10 intro-constructs bullet; there is no OOP-specific learning
standard at grade 11/12. OOP is language-feature material in BC.
*Feeder-link:* AP CSA.

## Unit 9 — Boolean Logic and Number Systems (binary/hex)

**Computer Studies 10 Content (verbatim):**
> "…Boolean operators, comparison operators, arithmetic operators…"
> (within the intro-constructs bullet)

**Computer Information Systems 11 Content (verbatim — data encoding):**
> "use of correct terminology to describe the units, rates, and encoding

*Syllabus note:* BC does **not** have an explicit binary/hex number-base

data encoding appears in CIS 11 (communications context). `syllabus-note`

## Unit 10 — Data, Databases and the Web

**Computer Studies 10 Content (verbatim):**
> "risks and rewards (data collection, personal information, privacy
> concerns, remote hacking, information as a commodity, …) associated
> with big data, multi-device connectivity, and the Internet of Things"

**Computer Information Systems 11 Content (verbatim):**
> "evolution of computer technology, including hardware, software,
> networks, and the Internet"; "installation and configuration of
> operating systems"; "proprietary versus open-source applications"

**Computer Information Systems 12 Content (verbatim):**
> "global and societal shifts resulting from emerging technologies, the
> Internet, and the ubiquity of online access"

*Syllabus note:* BC has **no relational-database/SQL learning standard**
in the ADST computer courses. "Data" appears as big-data/IoT
(Computer Studies 10) and as Internet/online-access shifts (CIS 11/12).
`syllabus-note` that databases proper are not a BC HS topic.

## Unit 11 — Networks and the Internet

**Computer Studies 10 Content (verbatim):**
> "wired and wireless computer networking (network cards, routers,
> switches, cables, modems, network types)"

**Computer Information Systems 11 Content (verbatim — strong networks coverage):**
> "network planning, setup, and diagnostics"; "key aspects of network
> protocols and standards"; "International Organization for
> Standardization (ISO) in the creation of open standards for networking;
> seven layers of the Open System Interconnection (OSI) reference model;
> 'interoperability' in the functioning of the Internet; four layers of
> the Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol (TCP/IP);
> model-wide area networks (WANs) and local area networks (LANs); logical
> and physical network topologies, including the segmentation of
> networks"

**Computer Information Systems 12 Content (verbatim, harder stream):**
> "design requirements of network devices, cabling, test equipment,
> management plans, operation manuals and documentation"; "deployment
> strategies, ongoing upgrades, maintenance, and security"; "network
> management tools, including security, imaging, backup, and remote
> access"; "functional and operational differences between hardware
> servers"; "virtual terminal applications"; "command line operations"

*Syllabus note:* BC's **CIS stream covers networks in real depth** (OSI
7-layer model, TCP/IP, WAN/LAN, topologies, server hardware) — deeper
and more infrastructure-oriented than Ontario's ICS3U/ICS4U, and
comparable in breadth to AP CSP Big Idea 4 but more hands-on/vocational.
`honors-flag` the CIS 12 deployment/management material.

## Unit 12 — Cybersecurity, Ethics and Society

**Computer Studies 10 Content (verbatim):**
> "computer security risks (malware, Trojans, viruses, phishing scams,
> identity fraud, ransomware)"; "preventive maintenance (… digital
> security measures, software updates, patches) of hardware and
> software"; "impacts of computers and technology on society (global
> communication, social media, e-commerce, … digital divide, …
> technology to assist people with diverse abilities)"; "ethical
> considerations (… copyright and fair use, … cyberbullying, white
> hat/black hat hacking, … e-waste, recycling, conflict mineral
> exploitation) of technology use, including cultural appropriation and
> environmental sustainability (e-waste, recycling and disposal, power
> consumption, renewable energy, server farms)"; "digital literacy (…
> safe online information sharing, cyberbullying, … ) and digital
> citizenship"; "impacts of technology use on personal health and
> wellness (cyber addictions; ergonomic issues; …)"

**Computer Information Systems 11/12 Content (verbatim):**
> "measures to secure the system against malicious exploits" (CIS 11);
> "awareness and understanding of digital security risks" (CIS 12);
> "environmental impacts of technology consumption" (CIS 12)

*Syllabus note:* BC's Computer Studies 10 has an unusually **broad
ethics/society/sustainability strand** (digital citizenship, e-waste,
conflict minerals, health/wellness, cultural appropriation) — the widest
of the four curricula on the social-impact axis. Maps well to CSTA `-IC`
and AP CSP Big Idea 5.

## Unit 13 — Software Development Process

**Computer Programming 11 Content (verbatim):**
> "design cycle"; "pair programming"; "tools to aid in the development
> process"; "inline commenting to document source code"; "use of test
> cases to detect logical or semantic errors"; "strategies to predict
> effects of code modification"

**Computer Programming 12 Content (verbatim, harder stream):**
> "Standardized source code documentation"; "Self-documenting code";
> "Collaboration tools for programming"; "Advanced pair programming";
> "User interface design"; "Error handling"; "Debugging tools";
> "Bug reports and feature requests from users"; "Interpersonal skills
> necessary for IT sector work"

*Syllabus note:* BC frames the whole of programming through the **design
cycle** Big Idea ("The design cycle is an ongoing reflective process").
Software-process material is woven through the Curricular Competencies
(prototype → test → iterate → share) rather than isolated to one strand.
