# Ontario SCH3U + SCH4U — Chemistry Extract

**Source:** *The Ontario Curriculum, Grades 11 and 12 — Science* (revised
2008). Two courses:
- **SCH3U** — Chemistry, Grade 11, University Preparation (pp. 90–103).
- **SCH4U** — Chemistry, Grade 12, University Preparation (pp. 104–117).

**Reused from cache.** The full Grades 11–12 Science document was already
pulled for the HS Physics grounding and is cached at
`rag/sources/on/science_11-12_physics.pdf` / `.txt` (it contains every
Grade 11–12 science course, including the chemistry courses). This
extract was sliced from the cached `.txt`; no new fetch was needed.

**Code form.** Ontario uses `{Course} {Strand}{Goal}.{n}`. Strands are
lettered **A–F**; within each strand the three overall-expectation goals
are numbered **1** ("Relating Science to Technology, Society, and the
Environment"), **2** ("Developing Skills of Investigation and
Communication"), **3** ("Understanding Basic Concepts"). So `SCH3U B3.3`
= SCH3U, strand B, goal 3, specific expectation 3. The **B3 / x3.x
"Understanding Basic Concepts" expectations are the citation-critical
content**; this extract captures them verbatim, plus the Overall
Expectations and the Big Ideas. Strand A (Scientific Investigation Skills
& Career Exploration) is common to both courses and is summarised, not
transcribed. *Sample issues / sample questions in the source are
illustrative and are omitted here.*

> SCH4C (Grade 12 College Preparation) is a separate, lower-depth course
> and is **out of scope** for these guides (the spec targets the
> University-stream SCH3U/SCH4U). Not extracted.

# SCH3U — Chemistry, Grade 11 (University Preparation)

**Course description (verbatim):** "This course enables students to
deepen their understanding of chemistry through the study of the
properties of chemicals and chemical bonds; chemical reactions and
quantitative relationships in those reactions; solutions and solubility;
and atmospheric chemistry and the behaviour of gases."

**Prerequisite:** Science, Grade 10, Academic.

**Strands:** A Scientific Investigation Skills · B Matter, Chemical
Trends, and Chemical Bonding · C Chemical Reactions · D Quantities in
Chemical Reactions · E Solutions and Solubility · F Gases and Atmospheric
Chemistry.

## Unit 1 — Atomic Structure & the Quantum Model  ·  Unit 2 — The Periodic Table & Periodic Trends  ·  Unit 3 — Chemical Bonding

### SCH3U Strand B — Matter, Chemical Trends, and Chemical Bonding

**Overall Expectations (verbatim):**
> B1. analyse the properties of commonly used chemical substances and
> their effects on human health and the environment, and propose ways to
> lessen their impact;
> B2. investigate physical and chemical properties of elements and
> compounds, and use various methods to visually represent them;
> B3. demonstrate an understanding of periodic trends in the periodic
> table and how elements combine to form chemical bonds.

**B2 / B3 specific expectations (verbatim):**
> B2.1 use appropriate terminology related to chemical trends and
> chemical bonding, including, but not limited to: atomic radius,
> effective nuclear charge, electronegativity, ionization energy, and
> electron affinity [C]
> B2.2 analyse data related to the properties of elements within a period
> (e.g., ionization energy, atomic radius) to identify general trends in
> the periodic table [AI]
> B2.4 draw Lewis structures to represent the bonds in ionic and molecular
> compounds [PR, C]
> B2.5 predict the nature of a bond (e.g., non-polar covalent, polar
> covalent, ionic), using electronegativity values of atoms [AI]
> B2.6 build molecular models, and write structural formulae, for
> molecular compounds containing single and multiple bonds (e.g., CO2,
> H2O, C2H4), and for ionic crystalline structures (e.g., NaCl) [PR, AI, C]
> B3.1 explain the relationship between the atomic number and the mass
> number of an element, and the difference between isotopes and
> radio-isotopes of an element
> B3.2 explain the relationship between isotopic abundance of an element's
> isotopes and the relative atomic mass of the element
> B3.3 state the periodic law, and explain how patterns in the electron
> arrangement and forces in atoms result in periodic trends (e.g., in
> atomic radius, ionization energy, electron affinity, electronegativity)
> in the periodic table
> B3.4 explain the differences between the formation of ionic bonds and
> the formation of covalent bonds
> B3.5 compare and contrast the physical properties of ionic and
> molecular compounds (e.g., NaCl and CH4; NaOH and H2O)

*Maps to:* Unit 1 (atomic/mass number, isotopes — B3.1, B3.2), Unit 2
(periodic law and trends — B2.1, B2.2, B3.3), Unit 3 (ionic vs covalent
bonding, Lewis structures, electronegativity — B2.4–B2.6, B3.4, B3.5).
Note Ontario's deeper electron-configuration / orbital treatment is in
**SCH4U C** (Unit 1 / Unit 3 honors), not SCH3U.

## Unit 5 — The Mole & Stoichiometry

### SCH3U Strand D — Quantities in Chemical Reactions

**Overall Expectations (verbatim):**
> D1. analyse processes in the home, the workplace, and the environmental
> sector that use chemical quantities and calculations, and assess the
> importance of quantitative accuracy in industrial chemical processes;
> D2. investigate quantitative relationships in chemical reactions, and
> solve related problems;
> D3. demonstrate an understanding of the mole concept and its
> significance to the quantitative analysis of chemical reactions.

**D2 / D3 specific expectations (verbatim):**
> D2.1 use appropriate terminology related to quantities in chemical
> reactions, including, but not limited to: stoichiometry, percentage
> yield, limiting reagent, mole, and atomic mass [C]
> D2.3 solve problems related to quantities in chemical reactions by
> performing calculations involving quantities in moles, number of
> particles, and atomic mass [AI]
> D2.4 determine the empirical formulae and molecular formulae of various
> chemical compounds, given molar masses and percentage composition or
> mass data [AI]
> D2.5 calculate the corresponding mass, or quantity in moles or
> molecules, for any given reactant or product in a balanced chemical
> equation as well as for any other reactant or product in the chemical
> reaction [AI]
> D2.6 solve problems related to quantities in chemical reactions by
> performing calculations involving percentage yield and limiting
> reagents [AI]
> D3.1 explain the law of definite proportions
> D3.2 describe the relationships between Avogadro's number, the mole
> concept, and the molar mass of any given substance
> D3.3 explain the relationship between the empirical formula and the
> molecular formula of a chemical compound
> D3.4 explain the quantitative relationships expressed in a balanced
> chemical equation, using appropriate units of measure (e.g., moles,

*Maps to:* Unit 5. Ontario treats stoichiometry, limiting reagents, and
percentage yield quantitatively in Grade 11 — deeper than NGSS HS-PS1-7,

(below); flag SCH4U-depth pH/Ka work honors-level for an SCH3U-only
student.

# SCH4U — Chemistry, Grade 12 (University Preparation)

**Course description (verbatim):** "This course enables students to

chemistry, the structure and properties of matter, energy changes and
rates of reaction, equilibrium in chemical systems, and electrochemistry."

**Strands:** A Scientific Investigation Skills · B Organic Chemistry ·

contrast NGSS HS-PS1-1/HS-PS1-3 (qualitative, no orbital filling rules,
no named IMFs).
