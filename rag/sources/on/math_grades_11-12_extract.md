# Ontario Grades 11–12 Math — Linear-Functions Extract

**Source:** `rag/sources/on/math_grades_11-12.pdf` (The Ontario
Curriculum, Grades 11 and 12: Mathematics, 2007 revision). Ontario
Ministry of Education.

**Scope:** Linear-relevant content from **MCR3U — Functions, Grade 11
University Preparation**. The Grade 12 courses (MHF4U, MCV4U, MDM4U)
do not have dedicated linear-functions content (covered in
prerequisite MPM1D/MPM2D) — they are noted at the end for completeness
of the Unit 1 Syllabus Map's downstream pointers.

> Conventions: Ontario uses **numbered expectations** (e.g. **1.1**,
> **1.2**) grouped under strand letters (**A**, **B**, **C**). The
> short citation form is `MCR3U A1.1`, `MCR3U C3.1`, etc. The strand
> letters are A · Characteristics of Functions, B · Exponential
> Functions, C · Discrete Functions, D · Trigonometric Functions.

## MCR3U — Functions, Grade 11 University Preparation

> Course description: *"This course introduces the mathematical
> concept of the function by extending students' experiences with
> linear and quadratic relations. Students will investigate
> properties of discrete and continuous functions, including
> trigonometric and exponential functions; represent functions
> numerically, algebraically, and graphically..."*

> **Prerequisite: Principles of Mathematics, Grade 10, Academic
> (MPM2D)** — i.e. MCR3U assumes the linear content of MPM1D/MPM2D
> as background and uses linear functions as the *base case* for
> the function-theory work.

### Strand A — Characteristics of Functions (linear-relevant subset)

**Overall Expectations** (linear-relevant only):

- A1. demonstrate an understanding of functions, their
  representations, and their inverses, and make connections between
  the algebraic and graphical representations of functions using
  transformations.

#### Sub-cluster A1 — Representing Functions

- **A1.1** explain the meaning of the term *function*, and
  distinguish a function from a relation that is not a function,
  through investigation of **linear and quadratic** relations using
  a variety of representations (tables of values, mapping diagrams,
  graphs, function machines, equations) and strategies (one-to-one
  or many-to-one mapping; vertical-line test).
- **A1.2** represent **linear and quadratic** functions using
  function notation, given their equations, tables of values, or
  graphs, and substitute into and evaluate functions
  *(e.g. evaluate $f\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\right)$, given
  $f(x) = 2x^2 + 3x - 1$)*.
- **A1.3** explain the meanings of *domain* and *range*, through
  investigation using numeric, graphical, and algebraic
  representations of the functions $f(x) = x$, $f(x) = x^2$,
  $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$, and $f(x) = \tfrac{1}{x}$; describe the domain
  and range of a function appropriately.
- **A1.4** relate the process of determining the *inverse* of a
  function to their understanding of reverse processes.
- **A1.5** determine the numeric or graphical representation of the
  inverse of a **linear or quadratic** function, given the numeric,
  graphical, or algebraic representation of the function, and make
  connections between the graph of a function and the graph of its
  inverse (e.g. the graph of the inverse is the reflection of the
  graph of the function in the line $y = x$).
- **A1.6** determine, through investigation, the relationship
  between the domain and range of a function and the domain and
  range of the inverse relation, and determine whether or not the
  inverse relation is a function.
- **A1.7** determine, using function notation when appropriate, the
  algebraic representation of the inverse of a **linear or
  quadratic** function, given the algebraic representation of the
  function *(e.g. the inverse of a linear function involves
  applying the inverse operations in the reverse order)*.
- **A1.8** determine, through investigation using technology, the
  roles of the parameters $a$, $k$, $d$, and $c$ in functions of
  the form $y = a f(k(x - d)) + c$, and describe these roles in
  terms of transformations on the graphs of $f(x) = x$ *(linear
  base case)*, $f(x) = x^2$, $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$, and
  $f(x) = \tfrac{1}{x}$ — translations; reflections in the axes;
  vertical and horizontal stretches and compressions.
- **A1.9** sketch graphs of $y = a f(k(x - d)) + c$ by applying one
  or more transformations to the graphs of $f(x) = x$, $f(x) = x^2$,
  $f(x) = \sqrt{x}$, and $f(x) = \tfrac{1}{x}$, and state the
  domain and range of the transformed functions.

#### Sub-cluster A2 — Solving Problems Involving Quadratic Functions (one linear-touching item)

- **A2.5** solve problems involving the **intersection of a linear
  function and a quadratic function** graphically and algebraically.

(A2.1–A2.4 and A3 are not linear-specific.)

### Strand B — Exponential Functions (linear comparisons)

- **B2.1** distinguish **exponential functions** from **linear and
  quadratic** functions by making comparisons in a variety of ways
  (e.g. graph $f(x) = x$, $f(x) = x^2$, and $f(x) = 2^x$; compare
  rate of change).

(Otherwise not linear-relevant — extracted only for the
linear-vs-exponential contrast.)

### Strand C — Discrete Functions (arithmetic sequences as linear growth)

- **C1.1** (paraphrase) recognize that arithmetic sequences
  correspond to *discrete linear functions*; $f(x) = 2x$ on the
  natural numbers is a discrete linear function (set of equally
  spaced points), and on the real numbers it's a continuous linear
  function (straight line).
- **C2.2** determine the formula for the general term of an
  arithmetic sequence (i.e. $t_n = a + (n - 1)d$).
- **C3.1** make and describe connections between **simple interest,
  arithmetic sequences, and linear growth**, through investigation
  with technology *(e.g. $f(x) = 500(1 + 0.05x)$)*.

(Geometric sequences and compound interest in C2 / C3 are
exponential, not linear.)

## Grade 12 University-stream courses — no dedicated linear strands

For reference; not needed for Unit 1 but useful for downstream Unit
pointers:

- **MHF4U — Advanced Functions, Grade 12 University.** Strands:
  Exponential and Logarithmic Functions; Trigonometric Functions;
  Polynomial and Rational Functions; Characteristics of Functions
  (composition, combining functions). No dedicated linear strand;
  linear is review.
- **MCV4U — Calculus and Vectors, Grade 12 University.** Strands:
  Derivatives and Their Applications; Geometry and Algebra of
  Vectors (2D and 3D vectors, lines in 3-space, planes). *Linear*
  in the sense of vector algebra (lines in 3-space; planes), **not**
  the linear-functions-of-one-variable sense covered by HS Math
  Unit 1.
- **MDM4U — Mathematics of Data Management, Grade 12 University.**
  Strands: Counting and Probability; Probability Distributions;
  Statistical Analysis. Not linear-functions content.
