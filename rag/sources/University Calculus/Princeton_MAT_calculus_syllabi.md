# Princeton — MAT 103 / 104 / 201 + ODE (scope anchor)

Grounds: MAT 103 -> Calc I (group A), MAT 104 -> Calc II (group B), MAT 201 -> Calc III (group C), ODE -> group D.
URL: https://www.math.princeton.edu/undergraduate/courses (course listings) and the Princeton course catalog.
Note: Princeton's introductory sequence is proof-aware and somewhat more theoretical than a typical state-school
sequence. Use this source mainly to calibrate RIGOR (epsilon-delta, careful theorem statements), not just topic lists.

## MAT 103 — Calculus I
Limits and continuity (intuitive and epsilon-delta), the derivative, differentiation rules, chain rule, implicit
differentiation, derivatives of transcendental functions, applications of the derivative (related rates,
linearization, extrema, Mean Value Theorem, curve sketching, optimization), antiderivatives and the definite
integral, the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
Maps to: A1-A8.

## MAT 104 — Calculus II
Techniques of integration (substitution, parts, trigonometric integrals and substitution, partial fractions),
improper integrals, applications of integration (area, volume, arc length), sequences and infinite series,
convergence tests, power series, Taylor series and remainder estimates, parametric and polar curves, complex
numbers. Known for a heavier emphasis on series and convergence rigor.
Maps to: B1-B8.

## MAT 201 — Multivariable Calculus (Calculus III)
Vectors and the geometry of space, vector-valued functions, functions of several variables, partial derivatives,
the gradient and directional derivatives, tangent planes and linear approximation, extrema and Lagrange
multipliers, multiple integrals (polar, cylindrical, spherical), vector fields, line integrals, Green's theorem,
surface integrals, the divergence theorem, and Stokes' theorem.
Maps to: C1-C8.

## Ordinary Differential Equations
Princeton does not run a single universal "intro ODE for everyone" course the way Georgia Tech runs MATH 2552;
ODE material is distributed across the applied sequence and engineering math (e.g., MAE/APC courses) and parts of
MAT 201/204. For grounding GROUP D, defer to MIT 18.03 and Georgia Tech MATH 2552 (both clean, canonical ODE
syllabi) as the primary scope anchors, and use Princeton only for rigor calibration.
Maps to: D1-D8 (via 18.03 / MATH 2552; flagged as an approximate Princeton mapping).
