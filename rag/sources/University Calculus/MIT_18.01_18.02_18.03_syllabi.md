# MIT OpenCourseWare — 18.01 / 18.02 / 18.03 (scope anchor)

Grounds: 18.01 -> our Calc I + Calc II (groups A, B). 18.02 -> Calc III (group C). 18.03 -> Calc IV / ODE (group D).
License: MIT OCW, CC BY-NC-SA. These are the canonical MIT single-variable, multivariable, and ODE courses.

## 18.01 Single Variable Calculus
URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-01sc-single-variable-calculus-fall-2010/

- **Unit 1 — Differentiation.** Rate of change, limits and continuity, definition of the derivative,
  derivatives of powers/polynomials, product and quotient rules, chain rule, implicit differentiation,
  derivatives of inverse, exponential, log, and trig functions, related rates, linear approximation.
- **Unit 2 — Applications of Differentiation.** Curve sketching (max/min, concavity, inflection), optimization,
  related rates, Newton's method, Mean Value Theorem, differentials.
- **Unit 3 — The Definite Integral and its Applications.** Definite integral as a limit of Riemann sums,
  Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, antiderivatives, areas between curves, volumes (disks/shells), average value,
  work, probability.
- **Unit 4 — Techniques of Integration.** Trigonometric integrals and substitution, integration by parts,
  partial fractions, improper integrals, L'Hopital's rule, numerical integration.
- **Unit 5 — Exploring the Infinite.** Sequences and series, convergence tests, power series, Taylor series.

Maps to: A1-A8 (Units 1-2 + start of 3), B1-B7 (Units 3-5).

## 18.02 Multivariable Calculus
URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-02sc-multivariable-calculus-fall-2010/

- **Unit 1 — Vectors and Matrices.** Vectors, dot and cross product, lines and planes, matrices, determinants,
  equations of planes, parametric equations of lines and curves.
- **Unit 2 — Partial Derivatives.** Functions of several variables, partial derivatives, gradient, directional
  derivatives, tangent planes, linear approximation, chain rule, max/min, second-derivative test, Lagrange
  multipliers, non-independent variables, differentials.
- **Unit 3 — Double Integrals and Line Integrals in the Plane.** Double integrals, polar coordinates, applications
  (mass, moments), change of variables, vector fields, line integrals, work, gradient fields and path independence,
  Green's theorem (both forms), flux, curl, divergence.
- **Unit 4 — Triple Integrals and Surface Integrals in 3-Space.** Triple integrals (rectangular, cylindrical,
  spherical), vector fields in 3D, flux, divergence theorem, line integrals in space, curl, Stokes' theorem,
  applications to physics.

Maps to: C1 (vectors), C2 (vector-valued/curves), C3-C5 (partials, gradient, Lagrange), C6 (multiple integrals),
C7 (line integrals, Green's), C8 (surface integrals, Stokes', divergence).

## 18.03 Differential Equations
URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-03sc-differential-equations-fall-2011/

- **Unit 1 — First-Order ODEs.** Direction fields, separable equations, linear first-order equations and
  integrating factors, substitution methods (Bernoulli, homogeneous), exact equations, existence and uniqueness,
  modeling (mixing, cooling, population, RC circuits), autonomous equations and stability.
- **Unit 2 — Second-Order Constant-Coefficient Linear ODEs.** Homogeneous equations and the characteristic
  equation, complex roots, repeated roots, undetermined coefficients, variation of parameters, harmonic
  oscillators, damping, resonance, RLC circuits, complex replacement and the exponential response formula.
- **Unit 3 — The Laplace Transform.** Definition and properties, transforms of derivatives, solving IVPs,
  unit step and delta functions, transfer functions, convolution.
- **Unit 4 — First-Order Systems.** Matrix methods, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, phase portraits, linear systems
  and stability, the matrix exponential. (18.03 then continues to Fourier series and intro PDEs, which are beyond
  our group D scope.)
- **Series solutions / numerical methods** appear in the OCW problem sets and supplementary notes (Euler's method,
  power-series solutions).

Maps to: D1-D2 (Unit 1), D3-D5 (Unit 2), D6 (Unit 3), D7 (Unit 4), D8 (numerical/series material).
Note: 18.03 Fourier series and PDEs are intentionally OUT of our group D scope (a standard intro-ODE course like
GT MATH 2552 ends near where group D ends).
