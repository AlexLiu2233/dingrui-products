# Georgia Tech — MATH 1551 / 1552 / 2551 / 2552 (scope anchor)

Grounds: 1551 -> Calc I (group A), 1552 -> Calc II (group B), 2551 -> Calc III (group C), 2552 -> Calc IV/ODE (group D).
URLs: https://catalog.gatech.edu/coursesaz/math/ and https://math.gatech.edu/courses
GT runs a modern "thread" calculus sequence; 1551/1552 split the single-variable year into differential then integral.

## MATH 1551 — Differential Calculus (2 credit hours)
Limits and continuity, the derivative and rules of differentiation (product, quotient, chain), implicit
differentiation, derivatives of exponential/logarithmic/trigonometric and inverse functions, linear approximation
and differentials, related rates, extrema and the Mean Value Theorem, curve sketching, optimization, L'Hopital's
rule, antiderivatives and an introduction to the definite integral.
Maps to: A1-A8.

## MATH 1552 — Integral Calculus (4 credit hours)
The definite integral and the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, techniques of integration (substitution, parts,
trigonometric integrals and substitution, partial fractions), improper integrals, numerical integration,
applications of integration (area, volume by disks/shells, arc length, work, average value), sequences and
infinite series, convergence tests, power series, Taylor and Maclaurin series, parametric equations and polar
coordinates.
Maps to: B1-B8 (with A8 overlap on the definite integral / FTC).

## MATH 2551 — Multivariable Calculus (4 credit hours)
Vectors and the geometry of space (dot/cross products, lines, planes, quadric surfaces), vector-valued functions
and motion (velocity, acceleration, curvature), functions of several variables, partial derivatives, the gradient,
directional derivatives, tangent planes and linear approximation, the multivariable chain rule, extrema and the
second-derivative test, Lagrange multipliers, double and triple integrals (including polar, cylindrical, and
spherical coordinates), change of variables, vector fields, line integrals, conservative fields, Green's theorem,
surface integrals, the divergence theorem, and Stokes' theorem.
Maps to: C1-C8.

## MATH 2552 — Differential Equations (4 credit hours)
First-order differential equations (separable, linear, exact, modeling and applications, direction fields,
existence and uniqueness), second-order linear equations (homogeneous with constant coefficients, undetermined
coefficients, variation of parameters, mechanical and electrical vibrations, resonance), the Laplace transform
and initial value problems, systems of first-order linear equations (eigenvalue method, phase plane analysis),
series solutions, and numerical methods (Euler, Runge-Kutta).
Maps to: D1-D8. This course is the cleanest 1:1 match for our entire group D.
