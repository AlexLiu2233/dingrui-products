# Alberta Mathematics 10C — Assessment Standards Extract (Units 1, 2, 4, 7)

**Source:** `rag/sources/ab/math10c_standards.pdf` (Outcomes with
Assessment Standards for Mathematics 10C, Alberta Education / Crown in
Right of Alberta, 2012). Raw text dump previously extracted via
`pdftotext -layout` lives at `rag/sources/ab/math10c_standards.txt`
(2400 lines). Sections below are verbatim quotes from that text dump
(re-flowed where pdftotext broke a sentence across columns; outcome
numbering and wording preserved exactly). Extraction date: 2026-05-29.

**Scope:** All Math 10C content that supports HS Math **Unit 1
(Linear)**, **Unit 2 (Quadratic) prereqs**, **Unit 4 (Rational and
Radical) prereqs**, and **Unit 7 (Right-Triangle Trigonometry)**. Math
10C is **Alberta's Grade 10 foundational course** that feeds both the
Math 20-1 (pre-calculus) and Math 20-2 (foundations) sequences. It
covers three topics: *Measurement*, *Algebra and Number*, and
*Relations and Functions*.

**Citation form.** Math 10C uses **topic + Specific Outcome number**
as the canonical short form. Topic short codes used here:
- **10C-M** = Math 10C Measurement
- **10C-AN** = Math 10C Algebra and Number
- **10C-RF** = Math 10C Relations and Functions

Each Specific Outcome (SO) carries a set of Achievement Indicators
(AI). Both **Acceptable Standard** and **Standard of Excellence**
performance levels are defined in the source.

---

## Standards-Setting Frame (verbatim)

> "Acceptable Standard: The acceptable standard of achievement in
> Mathematics 10C is met by students who receive a course mark between
> and including 50 percent and 79 percent. Typically, these students
> have gained new skills and a basic knowledge of the concepts and
> procedures relative to the general and specific outcomes defined for
> Mathematics 10C in the program of studies. These students can apply
> this knowledge to a limited range of familiar problem contexts."

> "Standard of Excellence: The standard of excellence for achievement
> in Mathematics 10C is met by students who receive a course mark at
> or above 80 percent. Typically, these students have gained a breadth
> and depth of understanding regarding the concepts and procedures, as
> well as the ability to apply this knowledge to a broad range of
> familiar and unfamiliar problem contexts."

## HS Math Unit 1: Linear Functions and Systems

**Math 10C — Relations and Functions:**

- **10C RF.1** *"Interpret and explain the relationships among data,
  graphs and situations."* [C, CN, R, T, V]
  - **AI 1.1** Graph, with or without technology, a set of data, and
    determine the restrictions on the domain and range.
  - **AI 1.5** Determine, and express in a variety of ways, the
    domain and range of a graph, a set of ordered pairs or a table of
    values.
  - *Standard note:* "Some ways students may describe the domain and
    range for a linear relation include: written or verbal
    descriptions; lists; **set builder notation**; e.g., $\{x \mid -2
    \le x \le 3, x \in \mathbb{R}\}$; **interval notation**;
    e.g., $(-2, 3)$. It is essential that students understand and are
    able to use set builder notation and interval notation
    effectively since these will be the primary ways of describing
    sets of numbers in future courses including Mathematics 20-1 and
    20-2."

- **10C RF.2** *"Demonstrate an understanding of relations and
  functions."* [C, R, V]
  - **AI 2.1** Explain, using examples, why some relations are not
    functions but all functions are relations.
  - **AI 2.2** Determine if a set of ordered pairs represents a
    function.
  - **AI 2.3** Sort a set of graphs as functions or non-functions.

- **10C RF.3** *"Demonstrate an understanding of slope with respect
  to: rise and run; line segments and lines; rate of change; parallel
  lines; perpendicular lines."* [PS, R, V]
  - **AI 3.1** Determine the slope of a line segment by measuring or
    calculating the rise and run.
  - **AI 3.3** Explain the meaning of the slope of a horizontal or
    vertical line.
  - **AI 3.4** Explain why the slope of a line can be determined by
    using any two points on that line.
  - **AI 3.5** Explain, using examples, slope as a rate of change.
  - **AI 3.8** Generalize and apply a rule for determining whether
    two lines are parallel or perpendicular.
  - **AI 3.9** Solve a contextual problem involving slope.

- **10C RF.4** *"Describe and represent linear relations, using:
  words; ordered pairs; tables of values; graphs; equations."*
  [C, CN, R, V]

- **10C RF.5** *"Determine the characteristics of the graphs of
  linear relations, including the: intercepts; slope; domain;
  range."* [CN, PS, R, V]

- **10C RF.6** *"Relate linear relations expressed in:
  slope–intercept form $(y = mx + b)$; general form
  $(Ax + By + C = 0)$; slope–point form $(y - y_1 = m(x - x_1))$ to
  their graphs."* [CN, R, T, V]
  - *Notes:* "When writing a linear equation in general form, e.g.,
    $Ax + By + C = 0$, the coefficients A, B and C must be integers
    and, by convention, A > 0."

- **10C RF.7** *"Determine the equation of a linear relation, given:
  a graph; a point and the slope; two points; a point and the
  equation of a parallel or perpendicular line, to solve problems."*
  [CN, PS, R, V]

- **10C RF.8** *"Represent a linear function, using function
  notation."* [CN, ME, V]
  - **AI 8.3** Determine the related range value, given a domain
    value for a linear function; e.g., if $f(x) = 3x - 2$, determine
    $f(-1)$.
  - **AI 8.4** Determine the related domain value, given a range
    value for a linear function; e.g., if $g(t) = 7 + t$, determine
    $t$ so that $g(t) = 15$.

- **10C RF.9** *"Solve problems that involve systems of linear
  equations in two variables, graphically and algebraically."*
  [CN, PS, R, T, V]
  - **AI 9.3** Determine and verify the solution of a system of
    linear equations graphically, with and without technology.
  - **AI 9.5** Determine and verify the solution of a system of
    linear equations algebraically.
  - **AI 9.6** Explain, using examples, why a system of equations may
    have no solution, one solution or an infinite number of
    solutions.

## HS Math Unit 2 (prereqs): Polynomial multiplication + factoring

**Math 10C — Algebra and Number (polynomial-factoring prereqs that
support quadratic-equation solving in Math 20-1):**

- **10C AN.4** *"Demonstrate an understanding of the multiplication
  of polynomial expressions (limited to monomials, binomials and
  trinomials), concretely, pictorially and symbolically."*
  [CN, R, V]
  - "(It is intended that the emphasis of this outcome be on binomial
    by binomial multiplication, with extension to polynomial by
    polynomial to establish a general pattern for multiplication.)"
  - **AI 4.5** Multiply two polynomials symbolically, and combine
    like terms in the product.
  - **AI 4.6** Generalize and explain a strategy for multiplication
    of polynomials.

- **10C AN.5** *"Demonstrate an understanding of common factors and
  trinomial factoring, concretely, pictorially and symbolically."*
  [C, CN, R, V]
  - *Notes:* "Students are expected to factor expressions such as
    $ax^2 + bx + c$, where $a \ne 1$, $a\in\mathbb{I}$. Polynomials
    involving two variables, e.g., $ax^2 + bxy + cy^2$, are included
    in this outcome."
  - **AI 5.1** Determine the common factors in the terms of a
    polynomial, and express the polynomial in factored form.
  - **AI 5.3** Factor a polynomial that is a difference of squares,
    and explain why it is a special case of trinomial factoring where
    $b = 0$.
  - **AI 5.7** Generalize and explain strategies used to factor a
    trinomial.
  - **AI 5.8** Express a polynomial as a product of its factors.

## HS Math Unit 4 (prereqs): Exponents + radicals

**Math 10C — Algebra and Number (radical / rational-exponent
foundation for Unit 4 in Math 20-1 and beyond):**

- **10C AN.1** *"Demonstrate an understanding of factors of whole
  numbers by determining the: prime factors; greatest common factor;
  least common multiple; square root; cube root."* [CN, ME, R]
  - **AI 1.5** Determine, using a variety of strategies, the square
    root of a perfect square, and explain the process.
  - **AI 1.6** Determine, using a variety of strategies, the cube
    root of a perfect cube, and explain the process.

- **10C AN.2** *"Demonstrate an understanding of irrational numbers
  by: representing, identifying and simplifying irrational numbers;
  ordering irrational numbers."* [CN, ME, R, V]
  - **AI 2.5** Express a radical as a mixed radical in simplest form
    (limited to numerical radicands).
  - **AI 2.6** Express a mixed radical as an entire radical (limited
    to numerical radicands).
  - **AI 2.7** Explain, using examples, the meaning of the index of a
    radical.
  - **AI 2.8** Represent, using a graphic organizer, the relationship
    among the subsets of the real numbers (natural, whole, integer,
    rational, irrational).

- **10C AN.3** *"Demonstrate an understanding of powers with integral
  and rational exponents."* [C, CN, PS, R]
  - **AI 3.1** Explain, using patterns, why $a^{-n}=\dfrac{1}{a^n}$,
    $a\ne 0$.
  - **AI 3.2** Explain, using patterns, why $a^{1/n}=\sqrt[n]{a}$,
    $n>0$.
  - **AI 3.3** Apply the exponent laws: $a^m\cdot a^n=a^{m+n}$;
    $a^m\div a^n=a^{m-n}$, $a\ne 0$; $(a^m)^n=a^{mn}$;
    $(ab)^m=a^mb^m$; $(a/b)^n=a^n/b^n$, $b\ne 0$; to expressions with
    rational and variable bases and integral and rational exponents.
  - **AI 3.4** Express powers with rational exponents as radicals and
    vice versa.

> *Out-of-scope note (10C-AN.2):* "Operations on radicals, other than
> those required for simplifying radicals, are not part of this
> outcome and will be introduced in Mathematics 20-1 and
> Mathematics 20-2."

## HS Math Unit 7: Right-Triangle Trigonometry

**Math 10C — Measurement:**

- **10C M.4** *"Develop and apply the primary trigonometric ratios
  (sine, cosine, tangent) to solve problems that involve right
  triangles."* [C, CN, PS, R, T, V]
  - *Notes:* "This outcome is the students' introduction to
    trigonometry; the primary trigonometric ratios are no longer
    developed in Grade 9. It is expected that students will develop
    the trigonometric ratios through hands-on activities that involve
    measurement and investigation. Prior knowledge from previous
    grade levels includes: the Pythagorean theorem (Grade 8); similar
    polygons (Grade 9)."
  - **AI 4.1** Explain the relationships between similar right
    triangles and the definitions of the primary trigonometric
    ratios.
  - **AI 4.2** Identify the hypotenuse of a right triangle and the
    opposite and adjacent sides for a given acute angle in the
    triangle.
  - **AI 4.3** Solve right triangles.
  - **AI 4.4** Solve a problem that involves one or more right
    triangles by applying the primary trigonometric ratios or the
    Pythagorean theorem.
  - **AI 4.5** Solve a problem that involves indirect and direct
    measurement, using the trigonometric ratios, the Pythagorean
    theorem and measurement instruments such as a clinometer or metre
    stick.
  - *Standard tier note:* the Standard of Excellence level requires
    students to "Provide a full solution for problems involving more
    than one right triangle" and to "Provide a detailed explanation
    of the solution."

> *Angle-of-elevation/depression note:* "The relationship between
> corresponding angles formed by two parallel lines and a transversal
> is not an outcome addressed in previous grades. As a result,
> teachers may need to explain the relationship between an angle of
> elevation and the corresponding angle of depression."
