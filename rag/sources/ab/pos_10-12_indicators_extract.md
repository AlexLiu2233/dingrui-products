# Alberta 10-12 Mathematics Program of Studies — HS Math Unit Extract

**Source:** `rag/sources/ab/pos_10-12_indicators.pdf` (The Alberta 10-12
Mathematics Program of Studies with Achievement Indicators, 2008,
Alberta Education / Crown in Right of Alberta). 108 pp PDF. Raw text
dump previously extracted via `pdftotext -layout` lives at
`rag/sources/ab/pos_10-12_indicators.txt` (4125 lines). Sections below
are verbatim quotes from that text dump (re-flowed where pdftotext
broke a single sentence across columns; outcome numbering and wording
preserved exactly).

**Scope:** Topic-slice from the **Math 10C → Math 20-1 → Math 30-1**
sequence (the "-1 / Pre-Calculus" path that articulates with first-
year calculus). Cross-references each Specific Outcome (SO) and the
key Achievement Indicators (AI) to its matching HS Math unit. The
"-2" sequence (Math 20-2, 30-2) and "-3" sequence (workplace math)
are out of scope for HS Math; they are noted in the table of contents
only.

**Citation form.** Alberta uses **course code + strand SO number** as
the canonical short form. The strand letters used here are:
- **10C-AN** = Math 10C Algebra and Number
- **10C-RF** = Math 10C Relations and Functions
- **10C-M**  = Math 10C Measurement
- **20-1-AN** = Math 20-1 Algebra and Number
- **20-1-RF** = Math 20-1 Relations and Functions
- **20-1-T**  = Math 20-1 Trigonometry
- **30-1-T**  = Math 30-1 Trigonometry
- **30-1-RF** = Math 30-1 Relations and Functions
- **30-1-PCB** = Math 30-1 Permutations, Combinations and Binomial Theorem

The full short form for, e.g., Math 30-1 Relations and Functions
Specific Outcome 12 is **`30-1 RF.12`**.

> Math 31 (the Alberta calculus elective) is **not** part of the
> 10-12 POS document; it has its own program of studies in
> `rag/sources/ab/math31.pdf`. See `math31_extract.md` for the Unit 15
> calculus citation.

## HS Math Unit 1: Linear Functions and Systems

**Math 10C — Relations and Functions (foundation):**

- **10C RF.1** *"Interpret and explain the relationships among data,
  graphs and situations."* [C, CN, R, T, V]
  - **AI 1.1** Graph, with or without technology, a set of data, and
    determine the restrictions on the domain and range.
  - **AI 1.2** Explain why data points should or should not be
    connected on the graph for a situation.
  - **AI 1.5** Determine, and express in a variety of ways, the
    domain and range of a graph, a set of ordered pairs or a table of
    values.
- **10C RF.2** *"Demonstrate an understanding of relations and
  functions."* [C, R, V]
  - **AI 2.1** Explain, using examples, why some relations are not
    functions but all functions are relations.
  - **AI 2.2** Determine if a set of ordered pairs represents a
    function.
  - **AI 2.3** Sort a set of graphs as functions or non-functions.
- **10C RF.3** *"Demonstrate an understanding of slope with respect to:
  rise and run; line segments and lines; rate of change; parallel
  lines; perpendicular lines."* [PS, R, V]
  - **AI 3.5** Explain, using examples, slope as a rate of change.
  - **AI 3.8** Generalize and apply a rule for determining whether two
    lines are parallel or perpendicular.
  - **AI 3.9** Solve a contextual problem involving slope.
- **10C RF.4** *"Describe and represent linear relations, using:
  words; ordered pairs; tables of values; graphs; equations."*
  [C, CN, R, V]
- **10C RF.5** *"Determine the characteristics of the graphs of linear
  relations, including the: intercepts; slope; domain; range."*
  [CN, PS, R, V]
- **10C RF.6** *"Relate linear relations expressed in: slope-intercept
  form (y = mx + b); general form (Ax + By + C = 0); slope-point form
  (y - y1 = m(x - x1)) to their graphs."* [CN, R, T, V]
- **10C RF.7** *"Determine the equation of a linear relation, given:
  a graph; a point and the slope; two points; a point and the equation
  of a parallel or perpendicular line, to solve problems."*
  [CN, PS, R, V]
- **10C RF.8** *"Represent a linear function, using function
  notation."* [CN, ME, V]
  - **AI 8.3** Determine the related range value, given a domain value
    for a linear function; e.g., if f(x) = 3x - 2, determine f(-1).
- **10C RF.9** *"Solve problems that involve systems of linear
  equations in two variables, graphically and algebraically."*
  [CN, PS, R, T, V]
  - **AI 9.5** Determine and verify the solution of a system of linear
    equations algebraically.
  - **AI 9.6** Explain, using examples, why a system of equations may
    have no solution, one solution or an infinite number of solutions.

## HS Math Unit 2: Quadratic Functions and Equations

**Math 10C — Algebra and Number (polynomial-factoring prereqs):**

- **10C AN.4** *"Demonstrate an understanding of the multiplication of
  polynomial expressions (limited to monomials, binomials and
  trinomials), concretely, pictorially and symbolically."* [CN, R, V]
  - "(It is intended that the emphasis of this outcome be on binomial
    by binomial multiplication, with extension to polynomial by
    polynomial to establish a general pattern for multiplication.)"
- **10C AN.5** *"Demonstrate an understanding of common factors and
  trinomial factoring, concretely, pictorially and symbolically."*
  [C, CN, R, V]
  - **AI 5.3** Factor a polynomial that is a difference of squares,
    and explain why it is a special case of trinomial factoring where
    b = 0.
  - **AI 5.7** Generalize and explain strategies used to factor a
    trinomial.

**Math 20-1 — Relations and Functions (quadratic core):**

- **20-1 RF.1** *"Factor polynomial expressions of the form:
  ax² + bx + c, a ≠ 0; a²x² - b²y², a ≠ 0, b ≠ 0;
  a(f(x))² + b(f(x)) + c, a ≠ 0;
  a²(f(x))² - b²(g(y))², a ≠ 0, b ≠ 0,
  where a, b and c are rational numbers."* [CN, ME, R]
- **20-1 RF.2** *"Graph and analyze absolute value functions (limited
  to linear and quadratic functions) to solve problems."* [C, PS, R, T, V]
- **20-1 RF.3** *"Analyze quadratic functions of the form
  y = a(x - p)² + q and determine the: vertex; domain and range;
  direction of opening; axis of symmetry; x- and y-intercepts."*
  [CN, R, T, V]
  - **AI 3.5** Determine the coordinates of the vertex for a quadratic
    function of the form y = a(x - p)² + q, and verify with or without
    technology.
  - **AI 3.8** Explain, using examples, how the values of a and q may
    be used to determine whether a quadratic function has zero, one or
    two x-intercepts.
- **20-1 RF.4** *"Analyze quadratic functions of the form
  y = ax² + bx + c to identify characteristics of the corresponding
  graph, including: vertex; domain and range; direction of opening;
  axis of symmetry; x- and y-intercepts and to solve problems."*
  [CN, PS, R, T, V]
  - **AI 4.1** Explain the reasoning for the process of completing the
    square as shown in a given example.
  - **AI 4.2** Write a quadratic function given in the form
    y = ax² + bx + c as a quadratic function in the form
    y = a(x - p)² + q by completing the square.
- **20-1 RF.5** *"Solve problems that involve quadratic equations."*
  [C, CN, PS, R, T, V]
  - **AI 5.2** Derive the quadratic formula, using deductive
    reasoning.
  - **AI 5.3** Solve a quadratic equation of the form
    ax² + bx + c = 0 by using strategies such as: determining square
    roots; factoring; completing the square; applying the quadratic
    formula; graphing its corresponding function.
  - **AI 5.5** Explain, using examples, how the discriminant may be
    used to determine whether a quadratic equation has two, one or no
    real roots; and relate the number of zeros to the graph of the
    corresponding quadratic function.
- **20-1 RF.6** *"Solve, algebraically and graphically, problems that
  involve systems of linear-quadratic and quadratic-quadratic
  equations in two variables."* [CN, PS, R, T, V]
- **20-1 RF.7** *"Solve problems that involve linear and quadratic
  inequalities in two variables."* [C, PS, T, V]
- **20-1 RF.8** *"Solve problems that involve quadratic inequalities
  in one variable."* [CN, PS, V]
  - **AI 8.1** Determine the solution of a quadratic inequality in one
    variable, using strategies such as case analysis, graphing, roots
    and test points, or **sign analysis**; and explain the strategy
    used.

## HS Math Unit 3: Polynomial Functions

**Math 30-1 — Relations and Functions:**

- **30-1 RF.11** *"Demonstrate an understanding of factoring
  polynomials of degree greater than 2 (limited to polynomials of
  degree ≤ 5 with integral coefficients)."* [C, CN, ME]
  - **AI 11.1** Explain how long division of a polynomial expression
    by a binomial expression of the form x - a, a ∈ I, is related to
    synthetic division.
  - **AI 11.2** Divide a polynomial expression by a binomial
    expression of the form x - a, a ∈ I, using long division or
    synthetic division.
  - **AI 11.3** Explain the relationship between the linear factors of
    a polynomial expression and the zeros of the corresponding
    polynomial function.
  - **AI 11.4** Explain the relationship between the remainder when a
    polynomial expression is divided by x - a, a ∈ I, and the value of
    the polynomial expression at x = a (**remainder theorem**).
  - **AI 11.5** Explain and apply the **factor theorem** to express a
    polynomial expression as a product of factors.
- **30-1 RF.12** *"Graph and analyze polynomial functions (limited to
  polynomial functions of degree ≤ 5)."* [C, CN, T, V]
  - **AI 12.2** Explain the role of the constant term and leading
    coefficient in the equation of a polynomial function with respect
    to the graph of the function.
  - **AI 12.3** Generalize rules for graphing polynomial functions of
    odd or even degree.
  - **AI 12.4** Explain the relationship between: the zeros of a
    polynomial function; the roots of the corresponding polynomial
    equation; the x-intercepts of the graph of the polynomial
    function.
  - **AI 12.5** Explain how the multiplicity of a zero of a polynomial
    function affects the graph.

## HS Math Unit 4: Rational and Radical Expressions

**Math 10C — Algebra and Number (radical foundation):**

- **10C AN.3** *"Demonstrate an understanding of powers with integral
  and rational exponents."* [C, CN, PS, R]
  - **AI 3.1** Explain, using patterns, why a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ, a ≠ 0.
  - **AI 3.2** Explain, using patterns, why a^(1/n) = ⁿ√a, n > 0.
  - **AI 3.3** Apply the exponent laws: (aᵐ)(aⁿ) = a^(m+n);
    aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = a^(m-n), a ≠ 0; (aᵐ)ⁿ = a^(mn); (ab)ᵐ = aᵐbᵐ;
    (a/b)ⁿ = aⁿ/bⁿ, b ≠ 0; to expressions with rational and variable
    bases and integral and rational exponents.
  - **AI 3.4** Express powers with rational exponents as radicals and
    vice versa.

**Math 20-1 — Algebra and Number (rational + radical expressions):**

- **20-1 AN.2** *"Solve problems that involve operations on radicals
  and radical expressions with numerical and variable radicands."*
  [CN, ME, PS, R]
  - **AI 2.5** Rationalize the denominator of a rational expression
    with monomial or binomial denominators.
  - **AI 2.8** Identify the values of the variable for which a given
    radical expression is defined.
- **20-1 AN.3** *"Solve problems that involve radical equations
  (limited to square roots)."* [C, PS, R]
  - "(It is intended that the equations will have no more than two
    radicals.)"
  - **AI 3.4** Explain why some roots determined in solving a radical
    equation algebraically are **extraneous**.
- **20-1 AN.4** *"Determine equivalent forms of rational expressions
  (limited to numerators and denominators that are monomials,
  binomials or trinomials)."* [C, ME, R]
  - **AI 4.2** Explain why a given value is **non-permissible** for a
    given rational expression.
- **20-1 AN.5** *"Perform operations on rational expressions (limited
  to numerators and denominators that are monomials, binomials or
  trinomials)."* [CN, ME, R]
- **20-1 AN.6** *"Solve problems that involve rational equations
  (limited to numerators and denominators that are monomials,
  binomials or trinomials)."* [C, PS, R]
  - "(It is intended that the rational equations be those that can be
    simplified to linear and quadratic equations.)"

**Math 20-1 — Relations and Functions (reciprocal graphs):**

- **20-1 RF.11** *"Graph and analyze reciprocal functions (limited to
  the reciprocal of linear and quadratic functions)."* [CN, R, T, V]
  - **AI 11.2** Identify, given a function f(x), values of x for which
    y = 1/f(x) will have vertical asymptotes; and describe their
    relationship to the non-permissible values of the related rational
    expression.

**Math 30-1 — Relations and Functions (radical + rational functions):**

- **30-1 RF.13** *"Graph and analyze radical functions (limited to
  functions involving one radical)."* [CN, R, T, V]
  - **AI 13.2** Sketch the graph of the function
    y - k = a√(b(x - h)) by applying transformations to the graph of
    y = √x, and state the domain and range.
- **30-1 RF.14** *"Graph and analyze rational functions (limited to
  numerators and denominators that are monomials, binomials or
  trinomials)."* [CN, R, T, V]
  - **AI 14.3** Explain the behaviour of the graph of a rational
    function for values of the variable near a non-permissible value.
  - **AI 14.4** Determine if the graph of a rational function will
    have an **asymptote or a hole** for a non-permissible value.

## HS Math Unit 5: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

**Math 30-1 — Relations and Functions:**

- **30-1 RF.7** *"Demonstrate an understanding of logarithms."*
  [CN, ME, R]
  - **AI 7.1** Explain the relationship between logarithms and
    exponents.
  - **AI 7.2** Express a logarithmic expression as an exponential
    expression and vice versa.
  - **AI 7.3** Determine, without technology, the exact value of a
    logarithm, such as log₂ 8.
- **30-1 RF.8** *"Demonstrate an understanding of the product,
  quotient and power laws of logarithms."* [C, CN, ME, R, T]
  - **AI 8.1** Develop and generalize the laws for logarithms, using
    numeric examples and exponent laws.
  - **AI 8.2** Derive each law of logarithms.
- **30-1 RF.9** *"Graph and analyze exponential and logarithmic
  functions."* [C, CN, T, V]
  - **AI 9.1** Sketch, with or without technology, a graph of an
    exponential function of the form y = aˣ, a > 0.
  - **AI 9.2** Identify the characteristics of the graph of an
    exponential function of the form y = aˣ, a > 0, including the
    domain, range, **horizontal asymptote** and intercepts.
  - **AI 9.5** Identify the characteristics of the graph of a
    logarithmic function of the form y = log_b x, b > 1, including the
    domain, range, **vertical asymptote** and intercepts.
  - **AI 9.7** Demonstrate, graphically, that a logarithmic function
    and an exponential function with the same base are **inverses** of
    each other.
- **30-1 RF.10** *"Solve problems that involve exponential and
  logarithmic equations."* [C, CN, PS, R]
  - **AI 10.1** Determine the solution of an exponential equation in
    which the bases are powers of one another.
  - **AI 10.2** Determine the solution of an exponential equation in
    which the bases are not powers of one another, using a variety of
    strategies.
  - **AI 10.4** Explain why a value obtained in solving a logarithmic
    equation may be **extraneous**.
  - **AI 10.5** Solve a problem that involves exponential growth or
    decay.
  - **AI 10.7** Solve a problem that involves logarithmic scales, such
    as the Richter scale and the pH scale.

## HS Math Unit 11: Combinatorics and the Binomial Theorem

**Math 30-1 — Permutations, Combinations and Binomial Theorem:**

- **30-1 PCB.1** *"Apply the fundamental counting principle to solve
  problems."* [C, PS, R, V]
  - **AI 1.2** Explain, using examples, why the total number of
    possible choices is found by **multiplying rather than adding**
    the number of ways the individual choices can be made.
- **30-1 PCB.2** *"Determine the number of permutations of n elements
  taken r at a time to solve problems."* [C, PS, R, V]
  - **AI 2.2** Determine, in **factorial notation**, the number of
    permutations of n different elements taken n at a time to solve a
    problem.
  - **AI 2.6** Explain, using examples, the effect on the total
    number of permutations when **two or more elements are
    identical**.
- **30-1 PCB.3** *"Determine the number of combinations of n
  different elements taken r at a time to solve problems."*
  [C, PS, R, V]
  - **AI 3.1** Explain, using examples, the difference between a
