FRQ 1MEDIUM 3.5 Energy BookkeepingCalculator

A $0.50~\mathrm{kg}$ block is released from rest on a frictionless curved ramp at height $h = 1.2~\mathrm{m}$ above a horizontal floor. After leaving the ramp it slides along the floor and compresses a spring of constant $k = 600~\mathrm{N/m}$ until it momentarily stops. After the spring releases the block, the block slides back across the same horizontal floor — but on the return trip the floor has kinetic-friction coefficient $\mu_k = 0.20$.

(a) Determine the speed of the block at the bottom of the ramp.
(b) Determine the maximum compression of the spring.
(c) On the return trip the block slides until friction brings it to rest. Find the distance the block slides before stopping.
(d) Determine the fraction of the original mechanical energy dissipated by friction.
FRQ 2MEDIUM 3.4 Potential Energy DiagramCalculator

A particle of mass $m = 2.0~\mathrm{kg}$ moves along the $x$-axis under the conservative potential $U(x) = \tfrac{1}{4}x^4 - 2x^2 + 3$ (J, with $x$ in m).

(a) Determine the force $F(x)$ acting on the particle.
(b) Locate every equilibrium position and classify each as stable or unstable. Justify your classification using $d^2U/dx^2$.
(c) The particle is released from rest at $x = 0$. Describe the subsequent motion and justify with energy reasoning. (Hint: compare $U(0)$ with $U$ at the nearest minima.)
(d) If instead the particle is released from rest at $x = 3$, determine its speed when it next passes through $x = 0$.
FRQ 3HARD 3.2 Variable Friction CoefficientCalculator

A $4.0~\mathrm{kg}$ block slides along a horizontal surface whose kinetic-friction coefficient varies with position: $\mu_k(x) = 0.10 + 0.05\,x$, where $x$ is in metres. The block enters $x = 0$ moving with speed $v_0 = 5.0~\mathrm{m/s}$.

(a) Express the magnitude of the friction force as a function of $x$.
(b) Set up an integral expression for the total work done by friction as the block slides from $x = 0$ to $x = d$. (Do not evaluate.)
(c) Evaluate the integral from (b) for $d = 4.0~\mathrm{m}$.
(d) Use the work–energy theorem to determine the block's speed at $x = 4.0~\mathrm{m}$. (Confirm the block has not stopped before reaching $x = 4$.)
FRQ 4HARD 3.3 Constant-Power CarCalculator
