Q1EASY7.2 Mass-Spring PeriodCalculator

$m = 0.50~\mathrm{kg}$, $k = 200~\mathrm{N/m}$. SHM period?

  • (A) $0.10~\mathrm{s}$
  • (B) $0.16~\mathrm{s}$
  • (C) $0.31~\mathrm{s}$
  • (D) $0.63~\mathrm{s}$
Answer: (C)
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k} = 2\pi\sqrt{0.50/200} = 2\pi(0.050) \approx 0.314~\mathrm{s}$$
Q2EASY7.5 Simple Pendulum PeriodCalculator

Simple pendulum, $L = 1.0~\mathrm{m}$, $g = 9.8~\mathrm{m/s^2}$.

  • (A) $1.0~\mathrm{s}$
  • (B) $2.0~\mathrm{s}$
  • (C) $4.0~\mathrm{s}$
  • (D) $6.3~\mathrm{s}$
Answer: (B)
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/g} = 2\pi\sqrt{1.0/9.8} \approx 2.01~\mathrm{s}$$
The classic 1-m seconds-pendulum result.
Q3EASY7.1 Defining SHMNo Calculator

An object undergoes simple harmonic motion when the net force on it is

  • (A) constant in magnitude and direction
  • (B) proportional to its speed and opposite in direction
  • (C) proportional to its displacement from equilibrium and opposite in direction
  • (D) proportional to the square of its displacement from equilibrium
Answer: (C)
SHM ⇔ $F = -kx$ (linear restoring force). Trap (B) is the damping law; (D) gives anharmonic motion.
Q4EASY7.4 Energy at AmplitudeNo Calculator

Block on a spring at $x = +A$. Its energy is

  • (A) entirely kinetic
  • (B) entirely potential
  • (C) half kinetic and half potential
  • (D) zero (the spring is fully relaxed)
Answer: (B)
At $x = \pm A$ the block is momentarily at rest (turning point), so $K = 0$ and $E = U = \tfrac{1}{2}kA^2$. Trap (D) confuses "no motion" with "no energy" — the spring is fully stretched here, not relaxed.
Q5MEDIUM7.3 Initial ConditionsNo Calculator

$x(t) = A\cos(\omega t)$. Velocity at $t = 0$?

  • (A) $0$
  • (B) $A$
  • (C) $A\omega$
  • (D) $-A\omega$
Answer: (A)
$$v(t) = \dot x = -A\omega\sin(\omega t),\quad v(0) = -A\omega\sin 0 = 0$$
At $t = 0$ the particle is at $x = A$ (turning point) — naturally at rest.
Q6MEDIUM7.4 Maximum SpeedCalculator

$m = 0.20~\mathrm{kg}$, $k = 50~\mathrm{N/m}$, $A = 0.10~\mathrm{m}$. Maximum speed?

  • (A) $0.5~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (B) $1.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (C) $1.6~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (D) $2.5~\mathrm{m/s}$
Answer: (C)
$$v_\max = A\omega = A\sqrt{k/m} = 0.10\sqrt{50/0.20} = 0.10\sqrt{250} \approx 1.58~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Q7MEDIUM7.5 Pendulum Length ScalingNo Calculator

Pendulum length increases by $4$×. New period vs. original?

  • (A) $T/2$
  • (B) $T$
  • (C) $2T$
  • (D) $4T$
Answer: (C)
$T \propto \sqrt{L}$, so $L \to 4L$ gives $T \to 2T$. Trap (D) takes the ratio linearly.
Q8MEDIUM7.2 Mass-Spring FrequencyCalculator

$m = 0.40~\mathrm{kg}$, $k = 100~\mathrm{N/m}$. Frequency?

  • (A) $0.4~\mathrm{Hz}$
  • (B) $1.6~\mathrm{Hz}$
  • (C) $2.5~\mathrm{Hz}$
  • (D) $6.3~\mathrm{Hz}$
Answer: (C)
$$f = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{k/m} = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{250} \approx 2.52~\mathrm{Hz}$$
Trap (D) reports $\omega = \sqrt{k/m}$ instead of $f$.
Q9MEDIUM7.4 Energy Fraction at $x = A/2$No Calculator

Fraction of $E$ that is kinetic at $x = A/2$?

  • (A) $1/4$
  • (B) $1/2$
  • (C) $3/4$
  • (D) $7/8$
Answer: (C)
$$\frac{U(A/2)}{E} = \frac{\tfrac{1}{2}k(A/2)^2}{\tfrac{1}{2}kA^2} = \tfrac{1}{4}$$
So $K/E = 1 - \tfrac{1}{4} = \tfrac{3}{4}$.
Q10MEDIUM7.5 Physical Pendulum (Rod)No Calculator

Uniform rod $M$, $L$, pivoted at one end. Period of small-amplitude swing?

  • (A) $2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{L}{g}}$
  • (B) $2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{L}{2g}}$
  • (C) $2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{2L}{3g}}$
  • (D) $2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{3L}{2g}}$
Answer: (C)
$T = 2\pi\sqrt{I/(Mgd)}$ with $I = \tfrac{1}{3}ML^2$ and $d = L/2$:
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{\tfrac{1}{3}ML^2}{Mg(L/2)}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{2L}{3g}}$$
Note this is faster than the simple pendulum of length $L$ (since $2/3 < 1$) — the mass is distributed closer to the pivot.
Q11MEDIUM7.3 Phase ConstantNo Calculator

$x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)$ with $x(0) = 0$ and $\dot x(0) > 0$. Phase $\phi$?

  • (A) $0$
  • (B) $+\pi/2$
  • (C) $-\pi/2$
  • (D) $\pi$
Answer: (C)
$x(0) = A\cos\phi = 0 \Rightarrow \phi = \pm\pi/2$. Then $v(0) = -A\omega\sin\phi > 0 \Rightarrow \sin\phi < 0 \Rightarrow \phi = -\pi/2$.
Q12MEDIUM7.4 Speed at EquilibriumNo Calculator

Speed at the equilibrium position $x = 0$ in SHM (amplitude $A$, angular frequency $\omega$)?

  • (A) $0$
  • (B) $A\omega$
  • (C) $kA$
  • (D) $A/\omega$
Answer: (B)
At $x = 0$ all energy is kinetic: $\tfrac{1}{2}m v_\max^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}kA^2 \Rightarrow v_\max = A\sqrt{k/m} = A\omega$. Trap (C) has wrong units (N, not m/s).
Q13MEDIUM7.5 Pendulum on the MoonNo Calculator

Pendulum: $T_\mathrm{E}$ on Earth ($g$). Moon's $g_\mathrm{M} = g/6$. New period?

  • (A) $T_\mathrm{E}/6$
  • (B) $T_\mathrm{E}/\sqrt{6}$
  • (C) $T_\mathrm{E}\sqrt{6}$
  • (D) $6\,T_\mathrm{E}$
Answer: (C)
$T \propto 1/\sqrt{g}$, so $T_\mathrm{M}/T_\mathrm{E} = \sqrt{g/(g/6)} = \sqrt{6}$. The pendulum swings slower on the Moon.
Q14HARD7.4 Where KE Equals PENo Calculator

Position where $K = U$ in SHM (amplitude $A$)?

  • (A) $x = A/2$
  • (B) $x = A/\sqrt{2}$
  • (C) $x = A\sqrt{3}/2$
  • (D) $x = A$
Answer: (B)
$K = U \Rightarrow U = E/2 \Rightarrow \tfrac{1}{2}k x^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}\bigl(\tfrac{1}{2}kA^2\bigr) \Rightarrow x^2 = A^2/2 \Rightarrow x = A/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707\,A$. Trap (A) is where $U = E/4$; (C) is where $U = 3E/4$.
Q15HARD7.5 Numerical Physical PendulumCalculator

Rod $L = 1.5~\mathrm{m}$ pivoted at one end; small-amplitude period?

  • (A) $1.6~\mathrm{s}$
  • (B) $2.0~\mathrm{s}$
  • (C) $2.5~\mathrm{s}$
  • (D) $3.1~\mathrm{s}$
Answer: (B)
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{2L}{3g}} = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{3.0}{29.4}} \approx 2.01~\mathrm{s}$$
Q16HARD7.3 Speed at $x = A/2$No Calculator

$v(A/2) / v_\max$?

  • (A) $1/2$
  • (B) $\sqrt{3}/2$
  • (C) $3/4$
  • (D) $1$
Answer: (B)
$v(x) = \omega\sqrt{A^2 - x^2}$ from energy conservation. At $x = A/2$:
$$\frac{v}{v_\max} = \frac{\sqrt{A^2 - A^2/4}}{A} = \sqrt{3/4} = \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 0.866$$
Reciprocal of the position-to-amplitude ratio in some sense — this is also the cosine identity since $K/E = 3/4$.
Q17HARD7.4 Total Energy ExpressionsNo Calculator

Statements I. $E = \tfrac{1}{2}kA^2$ and II. $E = \tfrac{1}{2}m\omega^2 A^2$. Which are correct?

  • (A) I only
  • (B) II only
  • (C) Both I and II
  • (D) Neither
Answer: (C)
Statement I: $E$ at $x = \pm A$ is all PE, $\tfrac{1}{2}kA^2$. Statement II uses $\omega^2 = k/m$:
$$\tfrac{1}{2}m\omega^2 A^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}m(k/m)A^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}kA^2$$
Identical expressions.
Q18HARD7.1 Differential Equation of SHMNo Calculator

$m\,\ddot x + kx = 0$. Angular frequency?

  • (A) $\dfrac{k}{m}$
  • (B) $\sqrt{\dfrac{k}{m}}$
  • (C) $2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{k}{m}}$
  • (D) $\dfrac{m}{k}$
Answer: (B)
Write as $\ddot x + (k/m) x = 0$ — the canonical SHM form $\ddot x + \omega^2 x = 0$ — so $\omega = \sqrt{k/m}$. Trap (A) is $\omega^2$; (C) is $\omega\cdot 2\pi$ (wrong units).

FRQ 1MEDIUM7.2 / 7.4 Mass-Spring SHMCalculator

$m = 0.40~\mathrm{kg}$, $k = 100~\mathrm{N/m}$, $A = 0.10~\mathrm{m}$, frictionless horizontal.

(a) $\omega$, $T$, $f$:
$$\omega = \sqrt{k/m} = \sqrt{100/0.40} = \sqrt{250} \approx 15.8~\mathrm{rad/s}$$
$$T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \approx 0.397~\mathrm{s},\qquad f = \frac{1}{T} \approx 2.52~\mathrm{Hz}$$
(b) Max speed and max acceleration:
$$v_\max = A\omega = 0.10(15.8) \approx 1.58~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$a_\max = A\omega^2 = 0.10(250) = 25~\mathrm{m/s^2}$$
(c) Total energy:
$$E = \tfrac{1}{2}kA^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(100)(0.01) = 0.50~\mathrm{J}$$
(d) At $x = 0.05~\mathrm{m}$ (i.e. $x = A/2$):
$$U = \tfrac{1}{2}k x^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}(100)(0.0025) = 0.125~\mathrm{J}$$
$$K = E - U = 0.500 - 0.125 = 0.375~\mathrm{J}$$
$K/E = 3/4$, matching Q9's analytic ratio.
FRQ 2MEDIUM7.3 Phase AnalysisCalculator

$x(t) = 0.20\cos(4 t + \pi/3)~\mathrm{m}$.

(a) Read off parameters: $A = 0.20~\mathrm{m}$, $\omega = 4~\mathrm{rad/s}$, $T = 2\pi/\omega = \pi/2 \approx 1.57~\mathrm{s}$, $\phi = \pi/3$.
(b) Derivatives:
$$v(t) = -A\omega\sin(\omega t + \phi) = -0.80\sin(4 t + \pi/3)~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$a(t) = -A\omega^2\cos(\omega t + \phi) = -3.2\cos(4 t + \pi/3)~\mathrm{m/s^2}$$
Note $a(t) = -\omega^2 x(t)$.
(c) At $t = 0$ ($\cos(\pi/3) = 0.5$, $\sin(\pi/3) = \tfrac{\sqrt 3}{2}$):
$$x(0) = 0.20(0.5) = 0.10~\mathrm{m}$$
$$v(0) = -0.80(0.866) \approx -0.69~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$a(0) = -3.2(0.5) = -1.6~\mathrm{m/s^2}$$
(d) First $t > 0$ with $x = 0$: $\cos(4t + \pi/3) = 0 \Rightarrow 4 t + \pi/3 = \pi/2 + n\pi$.
$$4 t = \pi/2 - \pi/3 = \pi/6 \;\Longrightarrow\; t = \pi/24 \approx 0.131~\mathrm{s}$$
FRQ 3HARD7.5 Physical PendulumCalculator

Rod $M = 0.50~\mathrm{kg}$, $L = 1.20~\mathrm{m}$ pivoted at one end, released from $\theta_0 = 30^\circ$ off vertical.

(a) Torque about the pivot from gravity (acting at the rod's CM at distance $L/2$): $\tau = -Mg(L/2)\sin\theta$. For small $\theta$, $\sin\theta \approx \theta$:
$$I\ddot\theta = -M g\,(L/2)\,\theta,\quad I = \tfrac{1}{3}ML^2$$
$$\ddot\theta = -\frac{Mg(L/2)}{(1/3)ML^2}\,\theta = -\frac{3g}{2L}\,\theta$$
Canonical SHM with $\omega^2 = 3g/(2L)$, so
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{2L}{3g}}$$
(b) Plug numbers:
$$T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{2(1.20)}{3(9.8)}} = 2\pi\sqrt{0.0816} \approx 1.79~\mathrm{s}$$
(c) Exact energy conservation: the rod's CM falls by $(L/2)(1 - \cos\theta_0)$ as $\theta$ goes from $\theta_0$ to $0$.
$$M g\,(L/2)(1 - \cos\theta_0) = \tfrac{1}{2}I\omega^2 = \tfrac{1}{6}ML^2\omega^2$$
$$\omega^2 = \frac{3g(1 - \cos\theta_0)}{L} = \frac{3(9.8)(1 - 0.866)}{1.20} \approx 3.28~\mathrm{rad^2/s^2}$$
$$\omega \approx 1.81~\mathrm{rad/s}$$
(d) Small-angle prediction $\omega_\max = \theta_0\sqrt{3g/(2L)}$ with $\theta_0 = \pi/6 \approx 0.524~\mathrm{rad}$:
$$\omega_\max^\mathrm{SHM} = 0.524\sqrt{3(9.8)/(2 \cdot 1.20)} = 0.524(3.50) \approx 1.83~\mathrm{rad/s}$$
Percent difference vs. exact: $(1.83 - 1.81)/1.81 \approx 1.1\%$. The small-angle approximation is excellent here — the $\theta - \sin\theta$ error at $30^\circ$ is just under 5% in the force, but the integrated motion happens to wash out into a $\sim 1\%$ peak-speed error.
FRQ 4HARD7.3 / 7.4 Vertical SpringCalculator

$m = 0.30~\mathrm{kg}$, $k = 60~\mathrm{N/m}$ vertical spring; block pulled down $A = 0.10~\mathrm{m}$ below the new equilibrium and released from rest.

(a) New equilibrium below natural length:
$$k x_0 = m g \;\Longrightarrow\; x_0 = \frac{m g}{k} = \frac{(0.30)(9.8)}{60} \approx 0.049~\mathrm{m}$$
(b) Let $y$ be displacement from the new equilibrium (downward positive). Net force at displacement $y$:
$$F_\text{net} = -k(x_0 + y) + m g = -k y$$
since $k x_0 = m g$. Newton's second law gives $m\ddot y = -k y$ — canonical SHM:
$$\omega = \sqrt{k/m} = \sqrt{60/0.30} = \sqrt{200} \approx 14.1~\mathrm{rad/s}$$
$$T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \approx 0.444~\mathrm{s}$$
The presence of gravity just shifts the equilibrium; it doesn't change $\omega$.
(c) Max speed and acceleration:
$$v_\max = A\omega = 0.10(14.1) \approx 1.41~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$a_\max = A\omega^2 = 0.10(200) = 20~\mathrm{m/s^2}$$
(d) $K = 3U \Rightarrow U = E/4$ (since $K + U = E$ and $K = 3U \Rightarrow 4U = E$).
$$\tfrac{1}{2}k y^2 = \tfrac{1}{4}\,\tfrac{1}{2}kA^2 \;\Longrightarrow\; y^2 = A^2/4 \;\Longrightarrow\; y = \pm A/2 = \pm 0.050~\mathrm{m}$$
Both signs are reached during the oscillation.
