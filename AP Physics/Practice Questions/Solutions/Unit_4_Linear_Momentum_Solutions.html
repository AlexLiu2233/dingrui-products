Q1EASY 4.1 Linear MomentumNo Calculator

A $0.50~\mathrm{kg}$ ball travels east at $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$. The magnitude of its momentum is

  • (A) $0.5~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$
  • (B) $2.0~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$
  • (C) $4.0~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$
  • (D) $8.0~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$
Answer: (B)
$$|\vec p| = m v = 0.50(4.0) = 2.0~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$$
Trap (D) doubles, (C) drops $m$, (A) drops $v$.
Q2EASY 4.2 ImpulseNo Calculator

A constant force of $10~\mathrm{N}$ acts on an object for $3.0~\mathrm{s}$. The magnitude of the impulse delivered to the object is

  • (A) $3.3~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
  • (B) $13~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
  • (C) $30~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
  • (D) $300~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
Answer: (C)
$$J = F\,\Delta t = 10(3.0) = 30~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$$
Trap (D) is $J g$ (extra factor of 10).
Q3EASY 4.3 Conservation of MomentumNo Calculator

Two carts of equal mass sit at rest on a frictionless track, in contact with a compressed spring between them. After the spring is released, cart A moves left at $2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$. Cart B then moves

  • (A) right at $1.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (B) right at $2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (C) left at $2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (D) right at $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
Answer: (B)
Total $\vec p$ was zero at rest and stays zero (no external horizontal force). With equal masses, equal-and-opposite speeds:
$$m(-2.0) + m\,v_B = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; v_B = +2.0~\mathrm{m/s}~\text{(right)}$$
Q4EASY 4.4 Inelastic vs. ElasticNo Calculator

In which type of collision is the total kinetic energy of the system not conserved?

  • (A) Elastic collision
  • (B) Perfectly inelastic collision
  • (C) Two billiard balls colliding glancingly without spinning
  • (D) Two pucks bouncing off a frictionless wall
Answer: (B)
Perfectly inelastic collisions have the maximum possible KE loss (the two bodies share a common final velocity). Elastic collisions preserve KE by definition; idealized billiard and frictionless-wall scenarios are taken as elastic. Momentum is conserved in every case — only KE differs.
Q5MEDIUM 4.2 Impulse from $F$–$t$ GraphNo Calculator

A force exerted on a $2.0~\mathrm{kg}$ object varies linearly from $0$ at $t = 0$ to $20~\mathrm{N}$ at $t = 0.40~\mathrm{s}$, then drops back to $0$ at $t = 0.80~\mathrm{s}$ (a triangular pulse). The object starts at rest. Its speed at $t = 0.80~\mathrm{s}$ is

  • (A) $2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (B) $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (C) $8.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (D) $16~\mathrm{m/s}$
Answer: (B)
Impulse is the area under the $F$–$t$ curve:
$$J = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\text{base}\,\cdot\,\text{height} = \tfrac{1}{2}(0.80)(20) = 8.0~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$$
Then $\Delta p = J \Rightarrow v = J/m = 8.0/2.0 = 4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$. Trap (C) uses a rectangular pulse (no half-factor).
Q6MEDIUM 4.2 Vector Change in MomentumNo Calculator

A $0.20~\mathrm{kg}$ ball strikes a wall horizontally at $5.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ and rebounds straight back at $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$. The magnitude of the impulse delivered by the wall to the ball is

  • (A) $0.20~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
  • (B) $1.0~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
  • (C) $1.8~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
  • (D) $9.0~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$
Answer: (C)
Treat incoming as $+x$. Then $v_i = +5$, $v_f = -4$ (rebound reverses direction).
$$\Delta p = m(v_f - v_i) = 0.20(-4 - 5) = -1.8~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$$
Magnitude is $1.8~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$. Trap (B) computes $m|v_f - v_i|/2$ (or treats the rebound speed as not changing sign).
Q7MEDIUM 4.4 Perfectly Inelastic CollisionNo Calculator

A $3.0~\mathrm{kg}$ cart moving at $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ collides with and sticks to a stationary $1.0~\mathrm{kg}$ cart on a frictionless track. The speed of the combined carts immediately after the collision is

  • (A) $1.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (B) $2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (C) $3.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (D) $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
Answer: (C)
$$m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2)\,v' \;\Longrightarrow\; v' = \frac{3.0(4.0)}{4.0} = 3.0~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Q8MEDIUM 4.3 RecoilNo Calculator

A $60~\mathrm{kg}$ skater stands at rest on frictionless ice and throws a $3.0~\mathrm{kg}$ ball horizontally at $8.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ relative to the ground. The skater's recoil speed is

  • (A) $0.20~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (B) $0.40~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (C) $1.6~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (D) $8.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
Answer: (B)
Total $\vec p$ stays zero:
$$60\,v_s + 3.0(8.0) = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; |v_s| = \frac{24}{60} = 0.40~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Q9MEDIUM 4.4 Equal-Mass Elastic CollisionNo Calculator

In a one-dimensional elastic collision, a moving puck strikes an identical stationary puck. Immediately after the collision,

  • (A) the moving puck stops and the target puck takes on its original velocity.
  • (B) both pucks move forward with half the original speed.
  • (C) the moving puck rebounds at full speed; the target puck stays at rest.
  • (D) both pucks come to rest, since momentum and KE both balance to zero.
Answer: (A)
For equal-mass 1-D elastic collisions, momentum and KE conservation force a full velocity exchange (use the standard $v_1' = ((m_1-m_2)/(m_1+m_2))v_1$ with $m_1 = m_2$ to get $v_1' = 0$, $v_2' = v_1$). Trap (B) would conserve momentum but lose KE; (C) would conserve KE but not momentum.
Q10MEDIUM 4.3 Two-Dimensional ConservationNo Calculator

Two pucks of equal mass collide and stick together. Just before the collision, puck 1 moves east at $3.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ and puck 2 moves north at $4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$. Immediately after the collision, the speed of the combined object is

  • (A) $1.5~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (B) $2.5~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (C) $3.5~\mathrm{m/s}$
  • (D) $5.0~\mathrm{m/s}$
Answer: (B)
Conserve momentum component-wise (mass $2m$ after):
$$2m\,v_x' = m(3) \;\Longrightarrow\; v_x' = 1.5,\qquad 2m\,v_y' = m(4) \;\Longrightarrow\; v_y' = 2.0$$
$$v' = \sqrt{1.5^2 + 2.0^2} = \sqrt{6.25} = 2.5~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Trap (D) directly takes $\sqrt{3^2 + 4^2}$ — that's the speed of the vector sum of velocities, but the combined mass dilutes each component by 2.
Q11MEDIUM 4.2 Average ForceCalculator

A $0.15~\mathrm{kg}$ baseball arrives at home plate moving horizontally at $40~\mathrm{m/s}$ and is hit straight back at $50~\mathrm{m/s}$. The contact between bat and ball lasts $1.5~\mathrm{ms}$. The magnitude of the average force exerted by the bat on the ball is closest to

  • (A) $1{,}000~\mathrm{N}$
  • (B) $4{,}000~\mathrm{N}$
  • (C) $9{,}000~\mathrm{N}$
  • (D) $14{,}000~\mathrm{N}$
Answer: (C)
Take incoming as $+$: $v_i = +40$, $v_f = -50$.
$$|\Delta p| = m|v_f - v_i| = 0.15(90) = 13.5~\mathrm{kg \cdot m/s}$$
$$\bar F = \frac{|\Delta p|}{\Delta t} = \frac{13.5}{0.0015} = 9{,}000~\mathrm{N}$$
Trap (A) treats $\Delta v = 10$ (just the speed difference, sign-blind); (D) drops the time-to-ms conversion.
Q12MEDIUM 4.4 KE Before vs. AfterNo Calculator

Two carts of equal mass collide head-on at the same speed and stick together. Compared with the total kinetic energy before the collision, the total kinetic energy immediately after the collision is

  • (A) the same
  • (B) half
  • (C) one-quarter
  • (D) zero
Answer: (D)
Total momentum before is $m v + m(-v) = 0$, so the combined object is at rest after sticking together. $K_f = 0$, $K_i = 2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} m v^2 = m v^2$, ratio $= 0$. All of the initial KE is dissipated. Trap (B) is the result for one cart moving and one at rest (max possible non-zero loss for perfect inelastic).
Q13MEDIUM 4.1 Momentum as a VectorNo Calculator

Object X has mass $2m$ and moves east at speed $v$. Object Y has mass $m$ and moves north at speed $2v$. The magnitudes of the two momentum vectors satisfy

  • (A) $|\vec p_X| > |\vec p_Y|$
  • (B) $|\vec p_X| < |\vec p_Y|$
  • (C) $|\vec p_X| = |\vec p_Y|$
  • (D) Cannot be determined without more information
Answer: (C)
$|\vec p_X| = 2 m v$ and $|\vec p_Y| = m(2v) = 2 m v$. Same magnitudes, different directions (perpendicular).
Q14HARD 4.4 Unequal-Mass Elastic CollisionNo Calculator

A puck of mass $M$ moves at speed $v$ and undergoes a one-dimensional elastic collision with a stationary puck of mass $3M$. Immediately after the collision, the velocities of the incoming and target pucks are, respectively,

  • (A) $+v/2$ and $+v/2$
  • (B) $-v/2$ and $+v/2$
  • (C) $-v$ and $+2v$
  • (D) $0$ and $+v/3$
Answer: (B)
Standard 1-D elastic-collision result with $m_2$ at rest:
$$v_1' = \frac{m_1 - m_2}{m_1 + m_2}\,v_1 = \frac{M - 3M}{4M}\,v = -\frac{v}{2}$$
$$v_2' = \frac{2 m_1}{m_1 + m_2}\,v_1 = \frac{2M}{4M}\,v = +\frac{v}{2}$$
The lighter incoming puck rebounds; the heavier target moves forward. Trap (A) would conserve momentum but not KE.
Q15HARD 4.3 Ballistic PendulumCalculator

A $0.020~\mathrm{kg}$ bullet moving horizontally at $300~\mathrm{m/s}$ strikes and embeds itself in a $2.98~\mathrm{kg}$ block hanging at rest at the end of a long string. Take $g = 9.8~\mathrm{m/s^2}$. The maximum height (above the lowest point of the swing) reached by the block-plus-bullet is closest to

  • (A) $0.05~\mathrm{m}$
  • (B) $0.10~\mathrm{m}$
  • (C) $0.20~\mathrm{m}$
  • (D) $0.41~\mathrm{m}$
Answer: (C)
Two-step problem. (i) Inelastic collision (momentum conserved):
$$v' = \frac{m_b v_b}{m_b + M} = \frac{0.020(300)}{3.00} = 2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$$
(ii) Swing (energy conserved):
$$h = \frac{v'^2}{2 g} = \frac{4.0}{19.6} \approx 0.204~\mathrm{m}$$
Trap (D) skips the collision step and uses $v_b^2/(2g)$ for the bullet alone (treating it as if its KE all became PE) — but most of the bullet's KE is dissipated in the collision.
Q16HARD 4.4 Two-Dimensional Elastic CollisionNo Calculator

Two pucks of equal mass $m$ undergo an elastic collision on a frictionless surface. Puck 1 moves east at speed $v$ and strikes puck 2, which is at rest. After the collision, puck 1 moves at $60^\circ$ north of east with speed $v/2$. The speed of puck 2 immediately after the collision is

  • (A) $v/4$
  • (B) $v/2$
  • (C) $\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\,v$
  • (D) $v$
Answer: (C)
Equal masses + elastic + one target initially at rest forces $\vec v_1' \perp \vec v_2'$ (a standard 2-D elastic result). Then KE conservation gives the magnitude:
$$v^2 = (v/2)^2 + v_2'^2 \;\Longrightarrow\; v_2'^2 = \tfrac{3}{4}v^2 \;\Longrightarrow\; v_2' = \tfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\,v$$
Cross-check the perpendicularity: puck 1 at $+60^\circ$ implies puck 2 at $-30^\circ$ from east; the angle between them is $90^\circ$. ✓
Q17HARD 4.2 Variable-Force ImpulseNo Calculator

A particle of mass $m$ initially at rest experiences a force $F(t) = \alpha t$ (with $\alpha$ a positive constant) along the $+x$ direction from $t = 0$ to $t = T$. The particle's speed at $t = T$ is

  • (A) $\dfrac{\alpha T}{m}$
  • (B) $\dfrac{\alpha T^2}{m}$
  • (C) $\dfrac{\alpha T^2}{2m}$
  • (D) $\dfrac{2\alpha T^2}{m}$
Answer: (C)
$$J = \int_0^T \alpha t\,dt = \frac{\alpha T^2}{2}$$
$$v = \frac{J}{m} = \frac{\alpha T^2}{2 m}$$
Trap (A) treats $F$ as if it were constant at $\alpha$ (units of N/s, not N); (B) skips the half-factor from integrating $t$.
Q18HARD 4.4 Fraction of KE LostNo Calculator

A projectile of mass $m$ moving at speed $v$ collides perfectly inelastically with a stationary target of mass $M$. The fraction of the projectile's original kinetic energy that is dissipated (converted to heat, sound, deformation, etc.) is

  • (A) $\dfrac{m}{m+M}$
  • (B) $\dfrac{M}{m+M}$
  • (C) $\dfrac{1}{2}$
  • (D) $0$
Answer: (B)
Final speed $v' = m v/(m+M)$. Energy fraction retained:
$$\frac{K_f}{K_i} = \frac{\tfrac{1}{2}(m+M)v'^2}{\tfrac{1}{2} m v^2} = \frac{m}{m+M}$$
Hence fraction lost is $1 - m/(m+M) = M/(m+M)$. Limits: $M \to 0$ (no target) gives no loss; $M \to \infty$ (immovable target) gives full loss.

FRQ 1MEDIUM 4.2 Variable-Force ImpulseCalculator

$F(t) = \alpha t$ with $\alpha = 24~\mathrm{N/s}$ acts on a $0.50~\mathrm{kg}$ block on a frictionless surface from $t = 0$ to $t = 0.50~\mathrm{s}$, starting at rest.

(a) Impulse from $t = 0$ to $0.50~\mathrm{s}$:
$$J = \int_0^{0.50} 24 t\,dt = \bigl[12 t^2\bigr]_0^{0.50} = 12(0.25) = 3.0~\mathrm{N \cdot s}$$
(b) Speed at $t = 0.50~\mathrm{s}$: $v = J/m = 3.0/0.50 = 6.0~\mathrm{m/s}$.
(c) Average force and peak.
$$\bar F = \frac{J}{\Delta t} = \frac{3.0}{0.50} = 6.0~\mathrm{N},\qquad F_\text{peak} = F(0.50) = 12~\mathrm{N}$$
For a linear ramp from zero, $\bar F = F_\text{peak}/2$, consistent with the triangular-pulse identity (area $= \tfrac{1}{2}\,\text{base}\,\cdot\,\text{height}$).
(d) Same impulse over $\Delta t = 0.10~\mathrm{s}$:
$$F = \frac{J}{\Delta t} = \frac{3.0}{0.10} = 30~\mathrm{N}$$
Safety implication. The change in momentum (e.g., bringing a passenger to rest in a crash) is fixed by the speed change. Air bags and padded dashboards extend the contact time $\Delta t$, lowering the peak force $\bar F = \Delta p/\Delta t$ — and hence the peak deceleration of the body — even though the total impulse $J$ is unchanged.
FRQ 2MEDIUM 4.3 Recoil & Center of MassCalculator

$60~\mathrm{kg}$ skater throws a $4.0~\mathrm{kg}$ medicine ball from rest on frictionless ice. Ball speed $6.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ (lab frame in parts a–c).

(a) Recoil. Total $\vec p = 0$ before and after:
$$60\,v_s + 4.0(6.0) = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; v_s = -\frac{24}{60} = -0.40~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Magnitude $0.40~\mathrm{m/s}$, opposite to the ball's direction.
(b) Momentum check:
$$p_\text{before} = 0,\qquad p_\text{after} = 60(-0.40) + 4.0(6.0) = -24 + 24 = 0~\checkmark$$
(c) Total KE after the throw:
$$K = \tfrac{1}{2}(60)(0.40)^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}(4.0)(6.0)^2 = 4.8 + 72 = 76.8~\mathrm{J}$$
Source: chemical/biological energy in the skater's muscles converted to mechanical KE during the throw. This is not a kinetic-energy-conserving process; it's a "controlled explosion" driven by an internal energy reservoir.
(d) Ball at $6.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ in the skater's frame. Let $v_s$ be the skater's lab velocity; the ball's lab velocity is $v_b = v_s + 6.0$. Momentum conservation:
$$60\,v_s + 4.0\,(v_s + 6.0) = 0 \;\Longrightarrow\; 64\,v_s = -24$$
$$v_s = -0.375~\mathrm{m/s},\qquad v_b = -0.375 + 6.0 = 5.625~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Compared with part (a) — same nominal "throw speed", but $v_s$ is slightly smaller in magnitude because the ball didn't have to reach $6~\mathrm{m/s}$ in the lab frame.
FRQ 3HARD 4.4 1-D Elastic CollisionCalculator

$m_1 = 0.30~\mathrm{kg}$ at $v_1 = +4.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ collides head-on elastically with $m_2 = 0.10~\mathrm{kg}$ at $v_2 = -2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$.

(a) Conservation equations:
$$m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = m_1 v_1' + m_2 v_2'$$
$$\tfrac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 + \tfrac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^2 = \tfrac{1}{2} m_1 v_1'^2 + \tfrac{1}{2} m_2 v_2'^2$$
(b) Solve. The two conservation laws are equivalent (for elastic 1-D) to "relative velocity reverses":
$$v_1' - v_2' = -(v_1 - v_2) = -(4.0 - (-2.0)) = -6.0$$
Combined with momentum $0.30 v_1' + 0.10 v_2' = 0.30(4.0) + 0.10(-2.0) = 1.0$:
$$0.30 v_1' + 0.10(v_1' + 6.0) = 1.0 \;\Longrightarrow\; 0.40 v_1' = 0.40 \;\Longrightarrow\; v_1' = 1.0~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$v_2' = v_1' + 6.0 = 7.0~\mathrm{m/s}$$
(c) KE check.
$$K_i = \tfrac{1}{2}(0.30)(16) + \tfrac{1}{2}(0.10)(4) = 2.40 + 0.20 = 2.60~\mathrm{J}$$
$$K_f = \tfrac{1}{2}(0.30)(1) + \tfrac{1}{2}(0.10)(49) = 0.15 + 2.45 = 2.60~\mathrm{J}~\checkmark$$
(d) CM velocity:
$$v_\text{cm} = \frac{m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2}{m_1 + m_2} = \frac{1.0}{0.40} = +2.5~\mathrm{m/s}$$
Unchanged by the collision because the collision forces are internal to the two-puck system; no external horizontal force acts during the brief contact, so total momentum (and therefore $v_\text{cm}$) is invariant.
FRQ 4HARD 4.4 2-D CollisionCalculator

$2.0~\mathrm{kg}$ puck east at $5.0~\mathrm{m/s}$ collides with stationary $3.0~\mathrm{kg}$ puck. After: $2.0~\mathrm{kg}$ puck at $30^\circ$ N of E, speed $2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$.

(a) Component momentum conservation. Let east be $+x$, north $+y$. After-collision puck-1 components: $v_{1x}' = 2.0\cos 30^\circ \approx 1.732$, $v_{1y}' = 2.0\sin 30^\circ = 1.0$.
$$2.0(5.0) = 2.0(1.732) + 3.0\,v_{2x}' \;\Longrightarrow\; v_{2x}' = \frac{10 - 3.464}{3.0} \approx 2.18~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$0 = 2.0(1.0) + 3.0\,v_{2y}' \;\Longrightarrow\; v_{2y}' = -\frac{2.0}{3.0} \approx -0.667~\mathrm{m/s}$$
(b) Speed and direction of the 3.0-kg puck:
$$|\vec v_2'| = \sqrt{2.18^2 + 0.667^2} = \sqrt{4.75 + 0.44} \approx 2.28~\mathrm{m/s}$$
$$\theta_2 = \arctan\!\left(\frac{-0.667}{2.18}\right) \approx -17^\circ~~\text{(i.e., $17^\circ$ south of east)}$$
(c) KE accounting.
$$K_i = \tfrac{1}{2}(2.0)(5.0)^2 = 25.0~\mathrm{J}$$
$$K_f = \tfrac{1}{2}(2.0)(2.0)^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}(3.0)(2.28)^2 = 4.0 + 7.80 = 11.8~\mathrm{J}$$
Inelastic. Fraction of $K_i$ lost: $(25.0 - 11.8)/25.0 \approx 0.53$ (53%).
(d) Center-of-mass velocity. Before:
$$v_\text{cm} = \frac{2.0(5.0) + 3.0(0)}{5.0} = 2.0~\mathrm{m/s~east}$$
After (x-component):
$$v_{\text{cm},x}' = \frac{2.0(1.732) + 3.0(2.18)}{5.0} = \frac{3.464 + 6.54}{5.0} \approx 2.0~\mathrm{m/s}$$
After (y-component):
$$v_{\text{cm},y}' = \frac{2.0(1.0) + 3.0(-0.667)}{5.0} = \frac{2.0 - 2.0}{5.0} = 0~\checkmark$$
$\vec v_\text{cm}$ is preserved because the collision involves only internal forces — no external horizontal force on the two-puck system.
