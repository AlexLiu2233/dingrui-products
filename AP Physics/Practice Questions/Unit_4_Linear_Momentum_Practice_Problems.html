FRQ 1MEDIUM 4.2 Variable-Force ImpulseCalculator

A horizontal force $F(t) = \alpha\,t$, with $\alpha = 24~\mathrm{N/s}$, acts along the $+x$ direction on a $0.50~\mathrm{kg}$ block that is initially at rest on a frictionless surface, from $t = 0$ to $t = 0.50~\mathrm{s}$.

(a) Set up an integral expression for the impulse delivered to the block from $t = 0$ to $t = 0.50~\mathrm{s}$, and evaluate it.
(b) Determine the speed of the block at $t = 0.50~\mathrm{s}$.
(c) Determine the average force exerted on the block over this interval, and compare it with the peak instantaneous force.
(d) The same impulse is now to be delivered as a constant force over a shorter interval of $0.10~\mathrm{s}$. Determine the magnitude of that constant force, and explain qualitatively why "spreading the impulse out over a longer time" is preferred for things like air bags or padded dashboards.
